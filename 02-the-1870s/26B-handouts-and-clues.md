# Chapter 26B — Handouts and Clues

These are the table-facing props for **The Yellowstone Line**. Hand them out when a player searches a pocket, opens a dispatch pouch, steals a packet, or asks for the thing men keep lying about. Every clue should live in more than one mouth, bag, or ledger. If the party misses one copy, another one should still surface before the chapter stalls.

## How to Use This Appendix

- Cut these into slips, cards, or text blocks.
- Read them aloud only if the players do not physically see them.
- Do not make any single handout load-bearing.
- If the party loses one, move the same fact into another carrier.

## 1. Mercer’s copied packet

> **Field copy, rough draft**
>
> Route grades west of Fort Ellis remain provisional.
>
> The easy line favors freight and town speculation.
>
> The hard line favors Army movement and slower road expense.
>
> Do not circulate beyond the official chest.

**What it tells the table:** someone is already splitting route knowledge.

**Best carriers:** Mercer, Keene, Vane, Reed’s spare case.

## 2. Bell’s pocket list

> **Crumpled scrap from Joe Bell’s coat**
>
> - pay slip, unsigned
> - pencil stub
> - one brass button
> - dispatch bag strap, cut
> - note: “do not trust the clerk”

**What it tells the table:** Bell saw trouble before he died.

**Best carriers:** Bell’s body, Mercer’s memory, a laundress who handled the coat, a trooper who searched the yard.

## 3. Keene’s confession scrap

> **A nervous hand’s confession, copied once and badly**
>
> I only copied what Mercer told me to copy.
>
> I did not mean to carry the whole thing off.
>
> Ask him. Ask Vane. Ask any of them if they will own it in daylight.

**What it tells the table:** the paper rot started as routine copying.

**Best carriers:** Keene, Mercer, a hidden duplicate, Rowe’s questions.

## 4. Pike’s freight chit

> **Freight office receipt**
>
> Forty sacks flour.
>
> Twelve barrels bacon.
>
> Six bales harness leather.
>
> Three team repairs, billed to Army account.
>
> Delivery to Fort Ellis horse yard, daylight only.

**What it tells the table:** Pike’s books and his loads never quite match.

**Best carriers:** Pike, a teamster, the horse-yard foreman, Hannah Calder’s ledger notes.

## 5. Vane’s private note

> **A clean line on clean paper**
>
> Keep the useful packet moving.
>
> The official copy is for men who still believe offices are honest.
>
> If the line breaks, Bozeman buys the first story and Helena buys the second.

**What it tells the table:** Vane is working the route as a future market.

**Best carriers:** Vane, Mercer, a copied envelope, a town booster in Bozeman.

## 6. Shows-the-Ford’s warning

> **Spoken through an interpreter or remembered by signal**
>
> Heavy wagons should not follow the low bend after rain.
>
> The far bank looks hard and is soft.
>
> If the white line insists on straightness, it will pay in horses.

**What it tells the table:** the Crow already knows the bad crossing.

**Best carriers:** Shows-the-Ford, LaRoche, a scout, a guide who listened well.

## 7. Hannah Calder’s account leaf

> **A ranch note written in a flat, strong hand**
>
> Nails were short paid.
>
> Flour was damp.
>
> Team hire was doubled after the survey men came.
>
> A line on paper makes other men hungry.

**What it tells the table:** the expedition is already distorting the local economy.

**Best carriers:** Hannah, her kitchen ledger, a hay dealer, a valley merchant.

## 8. Rowe’s order fragment

> **Army paper, folded twice**
>
> No civilian shall carry dispatches outside the appointed chain.
>
> Any man found copying route notes without authorization is to be held.
>
> Keep the horse line ready at first light.

**What it tells the table:** Rowe is trying to close the leak without admitting how bad it is.

**Best carriers:** Rowe, Duffy, a corporal, a recovered dispatch packet.

## Clue Redundancy Map

| Fact | Primary Carrier | Fallback Carriers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mercer is copying notes | Mercer | Keene, Bell’s body, Vane’s note |
| The route is being bent for profit | Vane | Reed’s maps, Pike’s chit, a town booster |
| Bell was killed for what he saw | Bell’s body | Mercer, a trooper, the cut strap |
| Pike is skimming Army loads | Pike’s books | Teamsters, Hannah’s account, the freight chit |
| The Crow know the bad crossing | Shows-the-Ford | LaRoche, a scout, a later river warning |
| The party can save or sell leverage | The packets | Rowe’s order, a copied map, Keene’s confession |

## What to Hand Out When

- **At Fort Ellis:** Rowe’s order fragment, Bell’s list, Mercer’s packet.
- **After the first killing:** Keene’s confession scrap, Pike’s freight chit.
- **At the ranch stop:** Hannah Calder’s account leaf.
- **When the Crow warning lands:** Shows-the-Ford’s warning.
- **At the reckoning:** any packet the party carried intact from the line.

## If a Handout Goes Missing

Move the truth.

- Lost Mercer copy? Put the same fact in Keene’s mouth.
- Lost Keene? Let Bell’s paper or Vane’s note carry the rot.
- Lost Pike’s chit? Make the freight balance visible in the horse yard.
- Lost the Crow warning? Let the bad ford speak for itself.

That is the rule of the chapter. No clue dies alone.
