# Reputation, Renown & Legend — Public Identity as Currency

> **STATUS: WORKSHOP MODULE.** Fame, infamy, rumor, awe, and public identity. Built from P2 capped pool, P14 memory, AC10 rumor economy, and AC11 propagation.

## 1. Origin

- **Source patterns:** P2, P4, P10, P14.
- **Operators:** Recalibration + Fusion.
- **Target psychology:** public action echoes.
- **Problem solved:** reputation should do more than add a generic social modifier.

## 2. Generic design space

Reputation is scoped by audience. Renown is spendable public weight; Rumor is unstable public story. A great act raises both useful and dangerous attention.

### Procedure

1. Define audience: village, court, guild, underworld, army, fandom, cult.
2. When a public deed lands, gain Renown +1 to +3 and roll or write a Rumor.
3. Spend Renown for access, morale, awe, or a social opening.
4. At thresholds, rumors mutate or propagate to connected audiences.
5. Infamy uses the same track with hostile effects.

### Rumor mutation

| D6 | Mutation | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Diminished | Renown gain reduced by 1. |
| 2 | Misattributed | Another actor shares or steals credit. |
| 3 | Exaggerated | Gain +1 Renown and +1 Heat. |
| 4 | Polarized | One audience improves, another worsens. |
| 5 | Weaponized | Rival gains leverage from the story. |
| 6 | Mythic | Renown becomes a persistent legend tag. |

### Renown gain triggers

Renown is always scoped by audience. A deed can gain Renown with one audience and Infamy with another.

| Deed | Renown | Complication |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Useful public help | +1 | creates expectation of future help |
| Dangerous victory witnessed by locals | +1 | rival hears of capability |
| Humiliating a named foe | +2 | foe gains revenge motive |
| Saving or ruining a community function | +2 | shifts faction Standing |
| Impossible/mythic deed | +3 | Heat/Threat +1 or rumor mutation |
| Betrayal, atrocity, taboo | Infamy +1 to +3 | access closes; fear opens other doors |

### Spend menu

Spending Renown reduces the pool unless the result says "stake" instead. Staked Renown returns if the scene succeeds and mutates if it fails.

| Spend | Cost | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **OPEN DOOR** | 1 | gain access to a person/place that respects the audience's stories |
| **COMMAND ROOM** | 1 | +1 die to a public social roll |
| **RALLY** | 2 | give allies +1 to resist fear/rout this scene |
| **CLAIM CREDIT** | 1 | attach your name to a deed; roll rumor mutation |
| **INTIMIDATE BY NAME** | 1-2 | replace threat proof with reputation proof |
| **CALL LEGEND** | 3+ | gain a one-scene legendary tag; always triggers mutation |

### Audience propagation

When Renown changes by 2+ with one audience, check connected audiences.

| Connection | Propagation |
| --- | --- |
| Allies/admirers | +1 Renown |
| Rivals/enemies | +1 Infamy or −1 Standing |
| Distant/neutral | no change unless rumor mutation says so |
| Dependent community | same shift, but with a demand attached |

### Public backlash table

Roll when a rumor mutates badly, Renown is staked and lost, or Infamy hits a threshold.

| D6 | Backlash | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Mockery | next Renown spend costs +1 |
| 2 | Demand | audience asks for proof, aid, or payment |
| 3 | Rival claim | someone contests the story |
| 4 | Scandal | roll social fallout or lose Influence |
| 5 | Dangerous follower | an admirer creates trouble |
| 6 | Enemy mobilizes | start Heat/Threat clock at 2 |

### Reputation design checks

- **Scope check:** name the audience every time.
- **Spend check:** if Renown grants access, it must be spent, staked, or create obligation.
- **Dual-face check:** big fame should produce both benefit and attention.
- **Solvent check:** Renown cannot bypass secret, intimate, or hostile systems unless the audience cares.

### Reputation architecture packs

| Pack | Track | Spend style | Backlash | Use for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Local Standing** | −3 to +3 per community | access and hospitality | gossip, demands | villages, crews |
| **Outlaw Infamy** | 0-10 Infamy | intimidation, underworld access | Heat, hunters | crime, westerns |
| **Heroic Renown** | 0-10 Renown | rally, awe, patronage | challengers, obligations | mythic fantasy |
| **Professional Name** | 0-6 Reputation | contracts, tools, apprentices | blacklisting | guild/career play |
| **Propaganda War** | audience clocks | shift factions | scandal, counter-narrative | politics, rebellion |

### Legend tags

When Renown reaches 6+ or a mythic deed mutates, create a Legend tag. A Legend tag is persistent but double-edged.

| Legend tag | Benefit | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Dragon-Slayer** | +1 to rally against monsters | monsters and glory-seekers seek you |
| **Oathbreaker** | +1 to intimidate the cautious | lawful patrons deny easy aid |
| **Ghost of the Road** | +1 to vanish in travel scenes | people doubt your honest testimony |
| **Saint-Touched** | +1 to comfort/heal believers | taboo violations become public scandal |
| **Butcher** | enemies may rout early | mercy and negotiation suffer |
| **Miracle-Worker** | desperate crowds seek you | refusing aid costs Renown or creates Heat |

### Reputation event table

Roll when Renown/Infamy crosses 3, 6, 9, or when a public deed is distorted.

| D66 | Event | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 11-16 | **Invitation.** | audience offers access with expectation. |
| 21-26 | **Petition.** | someone asks for help only your name can provide. |
| 31-36 | **Imitator.** | a follower copies you badly; Heat or Debt. |
| 41-46 | **Challenge.** | rival tests the legend publicly. |
| 51-56 | **Patronage.** | powerful actor offers support at a price. |
| 61-62 | **Slander.** | lose 1 Renown unless answered. |
| 63-64 | **Cult / following.** | gain asset and obligation. |
| 65 | **Legend hardens.** | create permanent tag; hard to change. |
| 66 | **Myth escapes you.** | public story demands a deed, sacrifice, or downfall. |

### Public action resolution

When PCs deliberately act for reputation:

1. Name audience and intended story.
2. Set deed risk: ordinary (+1), dangerous (+2), impossible (+3).
3. Roll the relevant scene normally.
4. On success, gain Renown and roll mutation if deed was +2 or more.
5. On failure, gain no Renown; choose backlash or Infamy.
6. If the deed was hidden, bank it as a Rumor until revealed.

### Worked mini-build

**Outlaw campaign:** Infamy is scoped by county. Spend 1 to intimidate a shopkeeper or gain underworld shelter; spend 2 to scatter a weak posse; spend 3 to force a named lawman to negotiate. At Infamy 6, hunters arrive. At 9, the governor posts a special reward. Cooling Infamy requires false death, pardon, or pinning the deed on a rival.

## 3. Pressure loop

Public deed -> Renown/Rumor -> access or backlash -> propagation -> new obligations.

## 4. Choices

| Choice | Grounded | Mythic | Psychology |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Scope | local audience | broad culture | reputation vs legend |
| Spend | access only | rules awe | social realism vs epic |
| Mutation | rare | frequent | stable name vs wild story |

## 5. Integration points

Pairs with Influence, Heat, Faction Web, Territory, and Companions.

## 6. Handshake

- **Prerequisites:** social standing or factions.
- **Inputs:** audiences, public deed triggers, spend menu.
- **Outputs:** Renown pool, Rumor tags, propagation events.
- **Touched systems:** social, GM procedures, faction/base, advancement.
- **Replaces or stacks:** replaces generic reputation bonuses; may stack with Influence only if spent for different effects.
- **Incompatibilities:** merge with Heat if public attention is purely hostile.

## 7. Failure modes

Universal reputation becomes a social solvent. Scope it by audience. Spending Renown must reduce the pool or create obligation.

## 8. Check notes

Use `24 §3` and `§5`. Watch for free access that bypasses economy, faction, or travel pressure.

## 9. Publication handoff

**Player rule:** Renown is what people think your name can do. Spend it where the audience knows the story.

## 10. Worked example — Mythic fantasy

Heroes gain Renown for monster-slaying. At 6 Renown a deed becomes Legend, granting awe in one region but attracting challengers.
