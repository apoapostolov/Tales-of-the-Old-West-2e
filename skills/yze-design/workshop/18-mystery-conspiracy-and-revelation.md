# Mystery, Conspiracy & Revelation — Truth Under Pressure

> **STATUS: WORKSHOP MODULE.** A deeper mystery framework for conspiracies, suspect webs, clue reliability, red herrings, and revelation pressure. Built from P3, P6, P14, AC2, and AC3.

## 1. Origin

- **Source patterns:** P3, P6, P14, P15.
- **Operators:** Domain Transfer + Fusion.
- **Target psychology:** progress with uncertainty.
- **Problem solved:** mysteries need failed rolls to complicate truth, not halt play.

## 2. Generic design space

The mystery has Revelations, Leads, Suspects/Factions, Pressure, and Reliability. Clues point to revelations; risky inference can jump ahead at a cost.

### Procedure

1. Write 3-5 required Revelations.
2. For each Revelation, write at least three Leads.
3. Give each Lead reliability: solid, partial, compromised.
4. Failed inquiry still grants a Lead, but adds time, Heat, Stress, or false framing.
5. Players may spend Clues to assert an inference; on risky inference, roll.
6. Pressure advances when time passes or wrong actors are alerted.

### Lead reliability

| Type | Use | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Solid | points clearly to a Revelation | hard to obtain |
| Partial | points to one aspect | needs combination |
| Compromised | true but poisoned by agenda | advances Pressure if trusted blindly |

### Mystery construction procedure

Use this before play. It is the part an AI can reskin most easily.

1. **Name the truth:** one sentence the scenario is really about.
2. **Write Revelations:** 3 for a one-shot, 5 for a short arc, 7+ only for campaign conspiracy.
3. **Attach Leads:** three Leads per Revelation: physical, social, and documentary/technical.
4. **Assign reliability:** one solid, one partial, one compromised per Revelation by default.
5. **Name pressure:** clock, Heat, victim count, ritual progress, rival investigation, disease spread.
6. **Write wrong-but-useful facts:** two red herrings that reveal something true about motive, method, or faction even when misleading.

### Inquiry activity menu

Each Investigation Phase, each PC chooses one activity.

| Activity | Roll | Success | Failure cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **SEARCH SITE** | Scout / Investigation | physical Lead | time, contamination, missed danger |
| **QUESTION** | Insight / Manipulation | social Lead | witness spooked, rumor spreads |
| **RESEARCH** | Lore / Records / Tech | documentary Lead | false context, rival notices |
| **TAIL / WATCH** | Stealth / Survival | movement Lead | Heat +1 or ambush |
| **ANALYZE** | Wits / Science / Magic | upgrade partial to solid | Stress or material consumed |
| **CONNECT** | social / faction skill | identify relationship edge | Debt or Standing shift |

### Revelation spend and inference checks

If using Clues, a Revelation costs 2 solid Clues or 1 solid + 2 partial. Compromised Clues count as solid only if the players accept their agenda cost.

**Risky inference:** when players jump ahead without enough Clues, roll the lead investigator's pool. Threshold equals missing Clues. On success, reveal the truth but add a cost. On failure, reveal why the inference was premature and fire Wrong Inference.

### Wrong Inference table

| D6 | Result | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Right target, wrong reason.** | reveal one true detail; pressure +1 |
| 2 | **Right faction, wrong actor.** | suspect warned; Standing −1 |
| 3 | **Trap sprung.** | next scene starts in bad position |
| 4 | **Evidence burned.** | one Lead becomes unavailable or compromised |
| 5 | **Rival leap.** | rival solves/uses one Revelation first |
| 6 | **Truth looks false.** | solid Lead becomes socially unusable until corroborated |

### Conspiracy web menu

For each node, write: public face, secret role, resource, fear, and what they do if pressured.

| Node type | Resource | Pressure response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Patron | money/access | cuts funding or buys silence |
| Enforcer | violence | attacks or intimidates witness |
| Scholar/technician | method | destroys notes or defects |
| Victim/witness | testimony | flees, recants, or asks protection |
| Front organization | legitimacy | legal/social counterattack |
| True power | impossible leverage | bargain, curse, disappear |

### Mystery design checks

- **Three-lead check:** every required Revelation has at least three routes.
- **Failure-progress check:** failed rolls still give a Lead or change the pressure state.
- **Reliability check:** compromised evidence tells truth plus agenda, not pure lies.
- **Pressure check:** every Investigation Phase changes time, Heat, victim safety, faction moves, or resources.

### Revelation map patterns

| Pattern | Structure | Best for | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Ladder** | Revelation 1 -> 2 -> 3 -> culprit | procedural case | can feel linear |
| **Hub** | all leads point to central secret | location mystery | central reveal may come early |
| **Web** | revelations connect factions/nodes | conspiracy | can sprawl |
| **Countdown** | each revelation stops one clock segment | thriller | pressure may overwhelm inquiry |
| **Palimpsest** | old truth under new truth | occult/history | needs clear evidence layers |

### Revelation type menu

| Type | Answers | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Actor** | who did/is doing it? | the patron, not the cult, funds it |
| **Method** | how is it done? | disease spreads through blessed wells |
| **Motive** | why? | revenge for dispossession |
| **Vulnerability** | how can it be stopped? | burn the ledger, not the bodies |
| **Location** | where next/final? | old observatory under courthouse |
| **Cost** | what stopping it demands? | save town or keep evidence intact |

### Lead generator

For each Revelation, write one from each row.

| Lead source | Solid | Partial | Compromised |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Physical | unique residue, wound, toolmark | common trace, direction, timing | planted object with one true detail |
| Social | honest witness, confession | rumor, overheard fear | witness protecting someone |
| Documentary | ledger, map, recording | torn note, bad copy | forged document with real signature |
| Supernatural/tech | vision, scan, spell, algorithm | distorted signal | entity/system shows truth for a price |

### Pressure clocks by genre

| Clock | Tick trigger | Fill consequence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Killer strikes again** | phase passes, wrong suspect accused | new victim, harder scene |
| **Ritual completes** | night falls, component secured | manifestation or corruption event |
| **Evidence decays** | delay, weather, contamination | Lead becomes compromised |
| **Conspiracy cleans house** | witness contacted, Heat rises | node disappears or attacks |
| **Public panic** | rumor spreads, failed social roll | mob, authority, scapegoat |

### Worked mini-build

**Medical mystery:** Truth: the cure is spreading the disease because the preservative is contaminated. Revelations: Method (preservative vector), Actor (well-meaning doctor), Motive (cover old negligence), Vulnerability (boil with bitterroot), Cost (admitting truth causes riot). Leads: physical residue in vials, social nurse testimony, documentary purchase ledger, compromised mayor's report. Pressure: patients worsen every phase; public panic clock ticks on failed announcements.

## 3. Pressure loop

Question -> Lead -> inference -> pressure/clarity -> new question.

## 4. Choices

| Choice | Procedural | Conspiracy | Psychology |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Revelations | linear | webbed | casework vs paranoia |
| Failure | time cost | hostile move | investigation vs thriller |
| Reliability | mostly solid | mixed | clarity vs uncertainty |

## 5. Integration points

Extends `05-investigation-and-clue-economy.md`; pairs with Heat, Faction Web, Reputation, and Projects.

## 6. Handshake

- **Prerequisites:** inquiry rolls or clue economy.
- **Inputs:** revelations, lead types, pressure trigger.
- **Outputs:** revelation map, suspect web, reliability tags.
- **Touched systems:** GM, social, travel, time, faction.
- **Replaces or stacks:** replaces linear clue chains; can stack with Clue economy if Clues buy inference, not access to basic leads.
- **Incompatibilities:** do not make players pay Clues for every required fact.

## 7. Failure modes

Hard clue gates stall play. Too many red herrings make players stop trusting the game. Every false lead should teach something or reveal pressure.

## 8. Check notes

Use `24 §3` and `§7`. Mandatory progress must survive failed rolls.

## 9. Publication handoff

**GM procedure:** Never hide the only path forward behind one roll. On failure, give the lead and make it cost.

## 10. Worked example — Occult conspiracy

Four Revelations expose a cult's patron, method, victim list, and ritual site. Compromised clues reveal truth but raise the cult's Attention clock.
