# Heat, Wanted & Exposure — Attention as a Threat

> **STATUS: WORKSHOP MODULE.** Escalating attention from law, surveillance, monsters, rivals, gods, or the public. Built from P2 inverted, P4 consequence thresholds, P14 memory, and AC5.

## 1. Origin

- **Source patterns:** P2, P4, P14, P15.
- **Operators:** Inversion + Fusion.
- **Target psychology:** success makes the world notice.
- **Problem solved:** risky action needs campaign memory beyond immediate failure.

## 2. Generic design space

Heat is a 0-10 track or die pool. It rises when characters act loudly, illegally, supernaturally, or visibly. At thresholds, the GM triggers attention: patrols, audits, hunters, rumors, divine omens.

**NEW CONCEPT — Success exposure:** Heat often rises when the characters succeed, because effective people attract attention.

### Procedure

1. When a loud/risky/signature act occurs, assign Heat +1 to +3.
2. At Heat 3/6/9, trigger an exposure event.
3. At Heat 10, force a confrontation, escape, cover-up, or campaign turn.
4. Reduce Heat by laying low, paying debt, framing another, moving territory, or resolving the attention source.

### Exposure table

| D6 | Event | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Trace | Someone knows where you were; future stealth −1 here. |
| 2 | Witness | A named NPC can identify you. |
| 3 | Patrol | A hostile force enters the next scene. |
| 4 | Pressure | Ally/contact demands distance or payment. |
| 5 | Escalation | Heat +1 and a stronger faction joins the response. |
| 6 | Net tightens | Start a 4-segment capture/exposure clock. |

### Heat gain menu

Assign Heat immediately when the act becomes knowable. If nobody could plausibly notice, bank the Heat as **latent** and reveal it when evidence is found.

| Trigger | Heat | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Quiet illegal act with trace | +1 | forged document, hacked door, trespass |
| Public disorder or visible anomaly | +1 | brawl, spell sign, alarmed crowd |
| Injury, corpse, explosion, major theft | +2 | add +1 if witnessed by authority |
| Signature method or calling card | +2 | also creates a Rumor if `14` is active |
| Forbidden power, military response, high-value target | +3 | immediate threshold check |
| Framing another actor | −1 to −3 | only if evidence supports it; creates Debt/Enemy |

### Cooldown actions

Cooldown is a downtime or mission action. It always costs opportunity: time passes, a job closes, a rival moves, or an ally asks payment.

| Action | Roll | Success | Failure |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **LAY LOW** | Stealth / Survival / Streetwise | Heat −1 | lose time; rival advances |
| **CLEAN EVIDENCE** | Craft / Tech / Investigation | Heat −1 or remove latent Heat | Heat +1 if discovered |
| **BRIBE / FAVOR** | Manipulation / Influence | Heat −2 | Debt +1 and Heat unchanged |
| **FRAME A RIVAL** | Deception / Performance | Heat −2, rival Heat +1 | Heat +1 and rival learns |
| **MOVE TERRITORY** | Travel / Contacts | ignore one threshold in old territory | lose access to a local function |
| **MAKE A DEAL** | social or pact roll | Heat resets to 3 | creditor gains a called debt |

### Threshold procedure

When Heat reaches a threshold, do not merely note it. Fire a concrete event.

1. **Heat 3 - Notice:** name who noticed and what they know.
2. **Heat 6 - Interference:** put an obstacle in the next scene.
3. **Heat 9 - Pursuit:** create a clock, hunter, warrant, audit, curse, or patrol route.
4. **Heat 10 - Confrontation:** force flight, trial, raid, bargain, cover-up, or showdown.

After a threshold fires, it cannot fire again until Heat drops below it and rises again, unless the GM escalates to the next threshold.

### Exposure families

Reskin the source of attention with one of these families.

| Family | Notice | Interference | Pursuit | Confrontation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Law** | witness statement | patrol/checkpoint | named investigator | arrest/raid/trial |
| **Corporate** | trace log | account freeze | recovery team | buyout/blackmail/kill order |
| **Monster** | scent/sign | stalking scene | lair minions | ambush/abduction |
| **Divine** | omen | taboo consequence | cult/inquisitor | miracle/curse/judgment |
| **Public** | rumor | crowd reaction | scandal clock | exile/mob/media storm |

### Heat design checks

- **Success check:** at least one common successful action should raise Heat, or the system only punishes failure.
- **Cooldown check:** every Heat reduction costs time, opportunity, Debt, Influence, or territory.
- **Threshold check:** every threshold creates a named actor or event.
- **Scope check:** Heat is scoped by territory/audience unless the genre is explicitly surveillance-saturated.

### Attention clocks

At Heat 6+, create a clock rather than repeating generic interference.

| Clock | Segments | Ticks when | Fills when |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Investigation** | 6 | evidence remains, witnesses talk | warrant, accusation, named hunter |
| **Manhunt** | 6 | PCs travel openly, call contacts | ambush or checkpoint net |
| **Containment** | 4 | supernatural/tech anomaly spreads | quarantine, purge, orbital strike |
| **Retaliation** | 4 | rival loses face/resources | revenge scene |
| **Public Scandal** | 6 | rumor mutates, social failure | exile, trial, patron withdrawal |

### Evidence tags

Heat becomes more playable when the GM names evidence instead of abstract attention.

| Evidence | How it is cleared | How it worsens |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Witness** | bribe, persuade, protect, discredit | killed or threatened witness creates +2 Heat |
| **Trace** | technical cleanup, forged trail | copied to stronger actor |
| **Body / wreckage** | hide, explain, consecrate | discovered by authority/family |
| **Signature method** | change tactics for a session | rival predicts next move |
| **Stolen object** | fence, return, alter | owner tracks it |
| **Omen / stain** | ritual cleansing | supernatural attention |

### Heat spend and pressure menu

Heat is usually bad, but it can be deliberately abuseed. This is dangerous and should always leave evidence.

| Player move | Heat cost/gain | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Make noise** | +1 Heat | draw guards/monster/faction away from an ally |
| **Claim responsibility** | +1 to +3 Heat | gain Renown/Infamy or force negotiations |
| **Burn identity** | set local Heat to 0 | lose contact, alias, safehouse, or legal existence |
| **Leak evidence** | +1 Heat | damage a rival or shift faction Standing |
| **Invite pursuit** | +2 Heat | choose where the confrontation begins |

### Worked threshold example

At Heat 3 after a warehouse theft, the GM names a dockworker witness. At Heat 6 after a public chase, the GM starts **Investigation 6** at 2 segments and introduces Inspector Vale. At Heat 9 after the crew uses their signature lightning tool, Vale gets a countermeasure and the next job begins with a checkpoint. At Heat 10, the crew must flee the district, frame a rival, or face a raid.

## 3. Pressure loop

Action -> exposure -> attention event -> cooldown choice -> future constraint.

## 4. Choices

| Choice | Low | High | Psychology |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cap | 6 | 10+ | short fuse vs campaign pressure |
| Trigger | only failures | successes and failures | stealthy vs paranoid |
| Cooldown | simple downtime | costly project/debt | pulpy vs hard-boiled |

## 5. Integration points

Pairs with chases, faction web, reputation, territory, social fallout, and mystery webs.

## 6. Handshake

- **Prerequisites:** GM consequence engine and faction/threat source.
- **Inputs:** Heat triggers, thresholds, cooldown actions.
- **Outputs:** Heat track, exposure events, clocks.
- **Touched systems:** GM procedures, travel, social, faction, base.
- **Replaces or stacks:** replaces ad hoc "guards arrive" rulings; stacks with faction Standing only through explicit events.
- **Incompatibilities:** if using Reputation, decide whether public fame increases or reduces Heat.

## 7. Failure modes

If Heat only punishes failure, players may become overcautious. If Heat cannot be reduced, it becomes doom. If cooldown is free, attention is cosmetic.

## 8. Check notes

Use `24 §3` and `§5`. Watch for loops where characters farm Heat to trigger rewards.

## 9. Publication handoff

**Player rule:** Heat measures how much the world is looking for you. Loud success, public violence, forbidden power, and sloppy exits raise it.

**GM procedure:** Trigger Heat events at thresholds, not by whim.

## 10. Worked example — Heist/crime

Heat rises +1 for alarms, +2 for bodies, +3 for public supernatural acts. At 6, a named investigator appears. Laying low for a Shift reduces Heat by 1 but costs a job opportunity.
