# Stress, Fear & Trauma — Pressure on the Self

> **STATUS: WORKSHOP MODULE.** A modular stress/fear rule set for horror, war, survival, occult, and high-pressure drama. Built from P5 resource dice, P4 fallout tables, P10 inner fire, and AC1 clocks.

## 1. Origin

- **Source patterns:** P5, P4, P10, P15.
- **Operators:** Domain Transfer + Fusion.
- **Target psychology:** mounting dread with recoverable scars.
- **Problem solved:** fear should pressure action without routinely removing the player from play.

## 2. Generic design space

Stress is a step die (D6-D12) or a track (0-6). A fear event rolls against Stress; bad results create Panic, Scars, or flight. Recovery requires safety, help, time, or a scene of grounding.

**NEW CONCEPT — Stress as depleting agency:** unlike physical harm, Stress primarily narrows choices before it wounds attributes.

### Procedure

1. When a frightening, exhausting, or shattering event occurs, set exposure: Safe / Risky / Terrible.
2. Roll the Stress die. On 1-2 it steps down; on Terrible exposure use 1-3.
3. At D6 + contraction, choose or roll Panic.
4. After the scene, convert unresolved Panic into a Scar or recover it with aid.
5. A Scar is a tag: it grants +1 in one narrow trigger and imposes −1 or Trouble in its mirror trigger.

### Panic fallout

| D6 | Panic | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Freeze | Lose your next FAST action. |
| 2 | Flee | Move away by the safest route or take 1 attribute damage resisting. |
| 3 | Lash out | Harm a bond, tool, or plan; GM offers two targets. |
| 4 | Fixate | You can act only toward one declared goal this Round. |
| 5 | Break cover | Add Heat/Threat +1 or reveal position. |
| 6 | Scar | Gain a Scar now; recover Stress one step after the scene. |

### Fear exposure checks

Use this table to decide whether Stress is rolled and how hard it bites. Do not roll for atmosphere alone; roll when the fiction asks the player to keep acting under pressure.

| Exposure | Trigger | Roll | On contraction | Extra cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Unease** | omen, corpse, uncanny sound, hostile silence | no roll unless already Stressed | none | GM may foreshadow |
| **Risky** | direct threat, isolation, helpless witness, first supernatural proof | roll Stress, contracts on 1-2 | mark Shaken | none |
| **Terrible** | body horror, massacre, betrayal, impossible revelation, friend taken | roll Stress, contracts on 1-3 | mark Shaken and choose Panic or take 1 WITS/EMPATHY damage | Heat/Threat +1 |
| **Overwhelming** | cosmic truth, possession, unit slaughter, certain death | roll Stress twice, keep worse | Panic immediately if either contracts | Scar if already Shaken |

**Shaken:** while Shaken, the next Stress contraction causes Panic even if the die has not reached D6. Clear Shaken with a grounding action, safe scene, or ally help.

### Grounding and recovery menu

During a scene, a character may use a FAST action to ground themselves or a SLOW action to ground someone at Arm's Length / Intimate social distance.

| Action | Roll | Success | Extra ⚔ |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **BREATHE** | WITS or relevant discipline | clear Shaken | give yourself +1 to resist the same source this scene |
| **ANCHOR** | EMPATHY / social skill | clear an ally's Shaken | ally steps Stress up one die on 2+ extra ⚔ |
| **NAME THE FEAR** | INSIGHT / LORE / command skill | identify the threat's tell | reduce next exposure by one step |
| **HOLD TOGETHER** | ENDURANCE / RESILIENCE | ignore one Panic effect until end of Round | take 1 damage to convert Panic into Shaken |
| **FLEE WELL** | MOVE / SURVIVAL | retreat without adding Heat/Threat | bring one ally with you |

Downtime recovery uses a project-style roll. Each safe Shift lets the character roll a recovery skill; success steps Stress up once. If a Bond, sanctuary, medicine, ritual, or confession is available, add +1 die. A failed pushed recovery roll does not step Stress down; instead it creates a Scar offer.

### Scar menu

When a Scar is gained, choose or roll. A Scar always has a **gift** and a **hook**. Use the gift once per session; the hook is always available to the GM when the trigger appears.

| D6 | Scar | Gift | Hook |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Watchful** | +1 to spot this threat type | cannot sleep safely near its signs |
| 2 | **Hard Shell** | +1 to resist intimidation/fear | −1 to comfort or trust others |
| 3 | **Vengeful** | +1 when acting directly against the source | must roll Stress to retreat from it |
| 4 | **Hollowed** | ignore first Shaken each session | recovery scenes require help |
| 5 | **Ritualized** | a personal rite clears Shaken | if denied the rite, exposure worsens |
| 6 | **Marked** | sense the threat before others | the threat senses you in return |

### Stress design checks

- **Frequency check:** no more than one Stress roll per character per scene unless the scene is explicitly a panic spiral.
- **Agency check:** every Panic result must leave the player a meaningful choice next Round.
- **Recovery check:** every session with Stress pressure should offer at least one plausible recovery opening.
- **Stacking check:** if Corruption exists, Stress handles immediate fear; Corruption handles long transformation.

### Stress architecture packs

Choose one pack before writing rules. This prevents the module from becoming a vague "fear penalty" bolted onto every genre.

| Pack | Starting Stress | Contraction | Panic trigger | Recovery | Use for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Cinematic Horror** | D8 | 1-2, Terrible 1-3 | D6 contraction or Shaken contraction | safe scene + ally | haunted house, monster movie |
| **Military Fatigue** | D10 | 1 on normal pressure, 1-2 under fire | D6 contraction only | rest Shift, medic, command | patrols, sieges, squad games |
| **Cosmic Dread** | D8 | 1-2, Overwhelming rolls twice | any contraction while Shaken | research + confession + sanctuary | mythos, occult investigation |

### Full trauma fallout table

Use when Panic would be too small for the moment: a character hit D6 contraction, suffered Overwhelming exposure, or failed a recovery project. Roll D66 or choose by severity. Rows 11-36 are scene scars, 41-56 are session scars, 61-66 are campaign scars.

| D66 | Trauma | Immediate effect | Recovery hook |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 11-12 | **White knuckles.** | Drop held item, clue, or tool. | retrieve it under pressure. |
| 13-14 | **Tunnel sight.** | You cannot assist others until you act on your own safety. | ally spends SLOW action to anchor you. |
| 15-16 | **Bad memory.** | −1 to the next roll using the same sense/source. | name what memory returned. |
| 21-22 | **Reflexive retreat.** | Move one range band/zone away; if impossible, take 1 damage. | choose who you leave exposed. |
| 23-24 | **Voice gone.** | Cannot command, cast verbally, or negotiate for one Round. | physical gesture or Bond restores voice. |
| 25-26 | **Shaking hands.** | gear/tag use at −1 until scene end. | take time to steady or accept worse position. |
| 31-32 | **Compulsion.** | GM names one irrational action; refuse by taking 1 Stress contraction later. | complete a grounding ritual. |
| 33-34 | **Flashback.** | lose next FAST action; reveal a past detail. | another PC may enter the memory with a Bond. |
| 35-36 | **Contagious fear.** | one nearby ally marks Shaken. | group rally clears both. |
| 41-42 | **Avoidance.** | gain Scar hook tied to a place, sound, person, or symbol. | confront it in safety. |
| 43-44 | **Mistrust.** | one Bond gains Strain +1. | repair with confession or sacrifice. |
| 45-46 | **Numbness.** | cannot spend Willpower/Faith for empathy/protection this scene. | accept help without paying it back. |
| 51-52 | **Night terrors.** | next rest does not recover one chosen track. | safe shelter or watchful ally. |
| 53-54 | **Obsession.** | must pursue one answer/threat before full recovery. | discover a useful truth. |
| 55-56 | **Somatic mark.** | one attribute recovery is blocked until treated. | healer, ritual, therapy, or time. |
| 61-62 | **Phobia.** | exposure to trigger is always at least Risky. | project to step down to Scar. |
| 63-64 | **Fractured identity.** | rewrite one belief/Bond; gain +1 against source, −1 elsewhere. | campaign-scale reconciliation. |
| 65 | **Catatonic break.** | character survives but exits the scene; allies must protect/extract them. | dedicated recovery arc. |
| 66 | **Taken by fear.** | character flees, submits, transforms, or becomes the threat's instrument. | only use if campaign allows exit or rescue arc. |

### Installation examples

**Occult investigation:** Stress is D8. Reading forbidden evidence is Risky; witnessing an impossible entity is Terrible; seeing the truth behind the cosmos is Overwhelming. Recovery requires a safe room and one true explanation of what was seen. Scars often grant occult sensitivity.

**War campaign:** Stress is D10 and contracts only under artillery, failed morale, massacre, or command betrayal. Panic is usually Freeze, Lash Out, or Fixate. Recovery requires a rest Shift, food, and either command reassurance or peer care.

**Monster survival:** Stress is D8. The monster's Tell causes Unease; direct sight is Risky; seeing it feed is Terrible. Panic results should create route, noise, separation, or gear loss more often than paralysis.

## 3. Pressure loop

Threat -> Stress roll -> narrowed agency -> Panic/Scar -> recovery scene or future trigger.

## 4. Choices

| Choice | Gentle | Harsh | Psychology |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Contraction | 1 on Risky, 1-2 Terrible | 1-2 Risky, 1-3 Terrible | unease vs terror |
| Recovery | one safe scene | downtime project | cinematic vs grinding |
| Scar polarity | boon + complication | mostly complication | resilient vs tragic |

## 5. Integration points

Hooks into harm, recovery, powers, monsters, and bonds. Pairs with `17` and `18`; shares caps with corruption if both exist.

## 6. Handshake

- **Prerequisites:** core rolls, recovery time, at least one social/bond or safety concept.
- **Inputs:** fear skill/attribute, recovery action, exposure scale.
- **Outputs:** Stress die, Panic results, Scars, optional sheet fields.
- **Touched systems:** harm, recovery, Willpower/Faith, GM threats.
- **Replaces or stacks:** replaces generic fear penalties; does not stack with a second panic table.
- **Incompatibilities:** merge with corruption or sanity systems if they use the same triggers.

## 7. Failure modes

Agency collapse is the main risk. Panic should change what a player can do, not make them sit out. Stress that never recovers becomes slow character death; Stress that always recovers becomes fake pressure.

## 8. Check notes

Use `24 §2`, `§6`, and `§7`. Worst case must remain playable unless the host game explicitly supports character exit.

## 9. Publication handoff

**Player rule:** When you face something that can break your nerve, roll your Stress die. If it contracts, fear has gotten under your skin. At D6, another contraction causes Panic.

**GM procedure:** Call for Stress only when the event changes choices. Do not roll for every eerie detail.

## 10. Worked example — Cinematic horror

D8 starting Stress, contraction 1-2, Terrible 1-3. Recovery requires a safe room scene. Scars give +1 against the same threat type but Trouble when protecting someone from it.
