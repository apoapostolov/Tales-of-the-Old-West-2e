# Compatibility Profiles — Agnostic YZE Design Targets

> **STATUS: FILLED.** FL and West are the source-backed calibration anchors. The profiles below are design archetypes for creating or extending YZE-style games when no exact host-game skill is loaded. Treat them as target postures, not claims about any specific published game.

## 1. How to use profiles

Choose one primary profile and, if needed, one secondary accent. The primary profile sets lethality, metacurrency, harm, loop weights, power density, campaign length, and failure modes. The secondary accent may add one workshop module or signature conflict, but must not overwrite the primary cost model.

Default anchors:

- **FL-like:** higher attrition, body/supply pressure, strong travel/base loops, deep optional systems.
- **West-like:** leaner machinery, Trouble/Faith-style pressure, documentary tone, stronger social/economic reality.

## 3. Profile details

### Survival Fantasy

- **Use when:** the world, weather, hunger, ruins, and home-building are the core adversary.
- **Defaults:** bane-self-harm or hybrid push; resource dice for supply; typed crit families; formal travel activity menu; stronghold or haven.
- **Workshop fit:** `04`, `07`, `09`, `13`, `15`, `17`.
- **Guardrail:** survival pressure must create choices, not daily accounting. Every travel roll should change route, supply, injury, information, or threat state.

### Cinematic Horror

- **Use when:** fear and revelation matter more than gear optimization.
- **Defaults:** stress/fear die, limited protected dial, strong panic/fallout tables, short recovery windows, escalating threat clock.
- **Workshop fit:** `07`, `10`, `11`, `17`, `18`.
- **Guardrail:** fear may constrain choices but should not routinely remove a player from play. Worst cases must produce playable scars, flight, separation, or transformation scenes.

### Mystery / Investigation

- **Use when:** play centers on discovering truth under pressure.
- **Defaults:** clue economy, revelation ladder, inquiry activity menu, red-herring cost, suspect/faction web, time or danger clock.
- **Workshop fit:** `05`, `06`, `14`, `18`.
- **Guardrail:** never hide mandatory progress behind a single failed roll. Failure should cost time, exposure, trust, or clarity.

### Space Opera

- **Use when:** crews, ships, factions, trade, diplomacy, and dramatic conflict all matter.
- **Defaults:** hybrid push, team momentum, ship as crewed org, faction edges, debt/favor economy, medium power/tech layer.
- **Workshop fit:** `02`, `06`, `11`, `12`, `13`, `15`, `19`.
- **Guardrail:** ship systems must give every PC a station or role; avoid pilot-only play.

### Post-Apoc Survival

- **Use when:** scarcity, salvage, territory, contamination, and community survival dominate.
- **Defaults:** supply resource dice, salvage as project fuel, heat/threat exposure, base upkeep, mutations/corruption optional.
- **Workshop fit:** `02`, `10`, `11`, `13`, `15`, `16`, `17`.
- **Guardrail:** scavenging must cost time, danger, contamination, or faction attention.

### Military / Squad

- **Use when:** missions, orders, morale, logistics, and squad cohesion are central.
- **Defaults:** command resource, morale die, wounds/fatigue, squad activity menu, mission clocks, base/chain-of-command obligations.
- **Workshop fit:** `04`, `10`, `12`, `13`, `16`, `19`.
- **Guardrail:** orders create constraints, not scripts. Every PC needs a meaningful tactical or moral decision.

### Mythic Fantasy

- **Use when:** heroes, divine bargains, epic magic, monsters, relics, and legends dominate.
- **Defaults:** high power layer, fate/glory protected dial, corruption/doom for forbidden force, relic growth, boss architecture.
- **Workshop fit:** `07`, `08`, `09`, `14`, `17`, `19`.
- **Guardrail:** mythic power must not delete travel, injury, economy, or social consequence; it should move costs to fate, doom, sacrifice, or obligation.

### Political / Social Drama

- **Use when:** status, scandal, obligation, relationships, and institutions are the battlefield.
- **Defaults:** influence pools, social combat as Situational, faction web, debt, reputation, relationship recovery.
- **Workshop fit:** `01`, `02`, `03`, `06`, `14`, `16`, `19`.
- **Guardrail:** preserve conversation. Use mechanics for high-stakes scenes, not every exchange.

### Heist / Crime

- **Use when:** crews take jobs, manage heat, spend prep, and escape consequences.
- **Defaults:** heat/wanted track, planning project, role menu, flashback-like protected dial only if costly, chase module.
- **Workshop fit:** `04`, `05`, `06`, `11`, `12`, `13`, `19`.
- **Guardrail:** prep gives options, not certainty. Heat must rise from success as well as failure.

### Mecha / Vehicle

- **Use when:** pilots and machines share the spotlight.
- **Defaults:** vehicle-as-character, station actions, pilot stress, repair projects, loadout feature grammar, signature duel/dogfight.
- **Workshop fit:** `10`, `12`, `13`, `17`, `19`.
- **Guardrail:** the vehicle layer must route consequences back to pilots, crew, base, and repairs.

### Cozy / Community

- **Use when:** repair, belonging, craft, seasons, and relationships are the core drama.
- **Defaults:** low lethality, fatigue/strain, community-as-character, projects, bonds, reputation, seasonal events.
- **Workshop fit:** `01`, `13`, `14`, `15`, `16`.
- **Guardrail:** low lethality is not no pressure. Use time, promises, scarce attention, weather, debt, and relationship strain.
