## Resolution conditions

_[What must be true for the investigation to be resolved?
Specify for each of the four minimum end-states.
See references/endings-and-sandbox.md.]_

### Ending 1 — Full resolution with legal proof

_[The party has gathered sufficient evidence to deliver to
an appropriate authority. What constitutes sufficient? Name
the specific evidence combination.]_

### Ending 2 — Truth without proof (open-loop ending)

_[The party knows who did it but cannot prove it legally.
What decision must they make? What does acting on unproven
knowledge cost?]_

### Ending 3 — Wrongful conclusion

_[The party acts on their best theory, which is wrong.
Who suffers? What evidence could have corrected them?
Is there a path back from this ending?]_

### Ending 4 — Clock-out failure

_[The clock advanced fully before the party solved the case.
What has the antagonist achieved? What is the world's new
state? Is the truth still findable?]_

## Pre-publish investigation audit

_[Complete before playtesting.]_

- [ ] The perpetrator's account has at least four steps,
      each leaving residue.
- [ ] Every critical fact has three minimum carriers in
      the distribution table.
- [ ] Arc I carriers are the most numerous.
- [ ] No clue text embeds a conclusion
      ("this is the murder weapon" is forbidden).
- [ ] The suspect web has at least one red herring with
      a falsifiable secret.
- [ ] Every non-final node has at least two exits.
- [ ] At least one hub node per act.
- [ ] The escalating timeline has at least two named
      clock advances.
- [ ] At least two of the four ending states are fully
      written (the open-loop ending must be one of them).
- [ ] The read-aloud has been tested aloud (it sounds
      right, not just reads right).
- [ ] No railroad phrases in the scene text.
      See references/anti-railroad.md.
