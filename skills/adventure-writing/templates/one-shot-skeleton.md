## Mandatory vs. optional sections for one-shots

_[A one-shot cannot carry all of architecture.md's sections._
_This note names what to keep and what to drop.]_

**Always keep:**

- Cold open
- Stakes (GM-only)
- NPC cast (five-sentence template for all named NPCs)
- Three clues per critical fact
- The central dilemma
- At least two endings

**Can compress or drop:**

- Historical frame (one sentence is enough)
- Full faction/track ledger (replace with a single pressure track)
- Full three-act rhythm (replace with the single-arc structure above)
- Sandbox exits (optional hooks only)
- Full reckoning structure (simplify to two to three scenes)

**Never drop (even compressed):**

- The three-clue rule
- The central dilemma (no clean option)
- At least two distinct endings
- Read-aloud for the opening and reckoning scenes

## Pre-publish one-shot audit

_[Complete before playtesting.]_

- [ ] Cold open is under 100 words and drops immediately
      into the scene.
- [ ] Cast has no more than 6 named NPCs.
- [ ] Every named NPC has the five-sentence template.
- [ ] Every critical fact has three minimum carriers.
- [ ] The dilemma has no clean option — both paths cost
      something real.
- [ ] At least two endings exist with distinct conditions.
- [ ] The adventure fits in the session length target
      (count scenes: a 3-hour session supports 4-6 scenes
      at mixed density).
- [ ] No railroad phrases in the scene text.
      See references/anti-railroad.md.
- [ ] Read-aloud has been tested aloud.
