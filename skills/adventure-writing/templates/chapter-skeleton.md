# Chapter Skeleton

A blank scaffold. Every section from `references/architecture.md`
in canonical order, with one-line guidance per slot. Copy the
file, rename, and fill.

The skeleton is system-agnostic. Combat blocks use placeholder
tags (e.g., `1 LIEUTENANT`, `4-6 RIDERS`); replace with system
stat blocks at the system-binding pass.

---

# Chapter Title

> _Cold open. Two to five sentences in italics. Sensory only.
> Land on a hard image. Closes on the chapter's central rot or
> pressure. See `references/read-aloud.md`._

## Stakes Paragraph

One paragraph. Names the era, the place, and the rot. States
what is at risk and who is doing the risking. Plain prose, no
italics, no read-aloud. The reader (the GM) finishes this
paragraph knowing what the chapter is _about_.

> Historical note: One to three sentences. Anchor the chapter
> to a specific year, season, and real-world (or setting-world)
> condition. See `references/historical-grounding.md`.

## The Shape of It

A short arc summary. Three to five sentences. Names the three
arcs without spoiling the dilemmas. Tells the GM the chapter's
rhythm: where the breath is, where the hammer falls, where
the reckoning sits.

## Hooks

Four to six hooks. Each one to two sentences. Each plausibly
attractive to a different party type (the mercenary, the
moralist, the territory-builder, the curious, the desperate).
Hooks are how the party arrives in the chapter, not what they
must do.

- **Hook one.** _Brief situation that pulls the party in._
- **Hook two.** _A different angle on the same chapter._
- **Hook three.** _Personal: an NPC the party already knows
  needs them here._
- **Hook four.** _Mercenary: pay and contract terms._
- **Hook five.** _Investigative: a rumor or anomaly._
- **Hook six.** _Adversarial: someone wants the party here
  for the wrong reasons._

## The Cast

Eight to twelve named NPCs. Each entry follows the
five-sentence template (see `references/npcs.md`).

### NPC Name (Role / Faction)

_Body and presence sentence. Defining quality sentence.
Mannerism sentence (observable). Speech register sentence.
Stance toward the party sentence._

> If killed early: _One-line robustness clause naming who
> picks up their function._

[Repeat for each named NPC. Group by faction or by arrival
order in the chapter.]

## Factions

Three to five factions. Each entry uses the Want / Method /
View triplet (see `references/factions-and-tracks.md`).

### Faction Name

- **Want:** _What this faction is trying to achieve in the
  chapter's window._
- **Method:** _How they pursue it. Specific behaviors._
- **View:** _What this faction thinks of the party by default,
  and what shifts that view._

[Repeat.]

## Consequence Ledger

Three or four named tracks. Never two, never five-plus. Each
track names a relationship or a resource, not a virtue. Each
moves on `+1` and `-1` based on player decisions, not
outcomes. See `references/factions-and-tracks.md`.

- **Track One** _(e.g., Faction A Respect)_: starts at `0`.
  Range `-3` to `+3`.
- **Track Two** _(e.g., Faction B Trust)_: starts at `0`.
  Range `-3` to `+3`.
- **Track Three** _(e.g., Local Goodwill)_: starts at `0`.
  Range `-3` to `+3`.
- **Track Four** _(optional, e.g., Resource Leverage)_:
  starts at `0`. Range `-3` to `+3`.

Coverage rule: every major NPC and every faction must touch
at least one track.

## Camp Scenes

A shelf of three to six brief scenes the GM can drop in
between hammer beats. Each one to three sentences. Used for
breath, characterization, and quiet faction work. See
`references/pacing-and-arcs.md`.

- **Scene name.** _Sensory hook. NPC who anchors it. What
  small information or texture it offers._
- _Repeat._

## Side Events

Four to eight short events the GM can pull in if the party
moves slowly, dawdles, or splits the party. Each one to three
sentences. Each ties to a track or a faction. See
`references/pacing-and-arcs.md`.

- **Event name.** _Trigger. Action. Consequence on tracks._
- _Repeat._

## Robustness Clauses

A short section listing the chapter's "if X" failsafes. Use
this as the central registry for clauses written into NPC
entries.

- **If [NPC A] dies in Arc I:** _Who picks up the function?
  How does the chapter shift?_
- **If the party refuses the opening contract:** _What still
  triggers the chapter? Through which carrier?_
- **If [Faction] withdraws:** _What replaces their pressure?_
- _Repeat for every fragile point._

See `references/anti-railroad.md`.

## Arc I — _Working title_

A breath arc. Establishes setting, cast, tracks, and the
opening rot. Ends on a choice or revelation, not a victory.
20-30% of chapter length.

### Arc I Pacing

One paragraph naming the arc's rhythm. How many scenes,
which are breath, which are hammer (typically one), where
the closing beat sits.

### Scene 1: _Title_

> _Read-aloud. Two to five sentences. Sensory. See
> `references/read-aloud.md`._

**Ground truth:** What is actually true in this scene that
the players don't yet know.

**NPC behavior:** What each present NPC is doing, wants, and
is likely to do if pressed.

**Player levers:** What the players can interact with — NPCs
to talk to, objects to inspect, exits to take, choices to
make.

**Branches** (minimum four):

- _If the players do X, then Y. Track shifts: [track ±1]._
- _If the players do A, then B. Track shifts: [track ±1]._
- _If the players refuse / leave, then C._
- _If the players do something not anticipated, the GM has
  the following levers to keep the scene live: ..._

**Residue:** What this scene leaves behind. NPC stance
changes, evidence on the ground, rumors that begin
circulating, faction reactions in the next scene.

[Combat block, if applicable:]

> **Enemies:** _Tags only. e.g., 1 LIEUTENANT, 4 HIRED RIDERS._
> **Area:** _One evocative line._
> **Terrain and Effects:** _Three to four sentences naming
> two to three tactical features and one moral pressure or
> environmental hazard._
> See `references/combat-encounters.md`.

### Scene 2: _Title_

[Repeat structure. Two to four scenes per arc.]

### Arc I Closing Beat

Names the choice, revelation, or loss that closes the arc.
Names track shifts. Names what the party carries into Arc II.

## Arc II — _Working title_

A hammer arc. Pressure rises. Multiple factions push at
once. Includes the chapter's central dilemma. 35-45% of
chapter length.

### Arc II Pacing

[As Arc I.]

### Scene 1: _Title_

[Scene structure repeats.]

### The Central Dilemma

A no-clean-option choice. See `references/dilemmas.md`. State
the dilemma in one paragraph. Then list four options:

- **Option A:** _What the players do. What they get. What it
  costs. Track shifts._
- **Option B:** _As above._
- **Option C:** _As above._
- **Option D:** _A third path the chapter is willing to
  accept if the players engineer it._

[Continue arc scenes.]

### Arc II Closing Beat

[As Arc I.]

## Arc III — _Working title_

A reckoning arc. Tracks cash out. The chapter's threads
converge. Multiple end-states are available based on the
ledger. 25-35% of chapter length.

### Arc III Pacing

[As Arc I.]

### Reckoning Scenes

Two to four scenes that converge on the reckoning. Players
make their final choices.

### Reckoning

The reckoning logic. Cash out the tracks (see
`references/endings-and-sandbox.md`).

> **If [Track One] dominates:** End-state name. Two-paragraph
> description of the new world.
> **If [Track Two] dominates:** End-state name.
> **If [Track Three] dominates:** End-state name.
> **If [Track Four] dominates, or no track dominates:**
> End-state name (often a "bad sandbox" — the chapter ends
> with the situation worse than it started).

Minimum four end-states. Each one paragraph minimum.

### Residue Beat

A short scene or paragraph after the reckoning. The chapter
catches its breath. A named NPC speaks the human cost. The
players see what they made. See `references/endings-and-sandbox.md`.

## Sandbox Carry-Forward

Three to seven directions the players can take from the
chapter's end. Mix of safe, opportunity, threat, and
mystery. Each one to two sentences.

- **Direction one (safe):** _Where they can rest, who hosts
  them._
- **Direction two (opportunity):** _A faction that wants
  them, a contract that opens._
- **Direction three (threat):** _Who hunts them now. What
  the consequences set loose._
- **Direction four (mystery):** _An open thread the chapter
  did not close._
- _Continue._

## Rewards

Tangible and intangible. List both.

**Tangible:**

- _Cash, gear, named items, stat-block-relevant rewards._

**Intangible:**

- _Track-based reputation. NPC alliances. Open invitations.
  Standing debts. Information._

## Open Threads

A short list of plot strands the chapter deliberately did not
resolve. Each one to two sentences. Each is a hook for a
later chapter.

- _Thread one._
- _Thread two._
- _Thread three._

## GM Notes

A closing section in the chapter's voice. Two to four
paragraphs. Tells the GM:

- The chapter's tone and what to lean into.
- Common ways the chapter goes sideways and how to ride
  that out.
- What to cut for short sessions, what to add for long ones.
- The chapter's central question and how to let the table
  answer it.

No prescriptions. The GM is the final arbiter.
