# Montana Campaign 1873 - Master Tracker

This document is the master plan for building a massive sandbox campaign supplement for Montana Territory in 1873.

## Editorial Rules

- Match the practical chapter usability of the New Mexico campaign chapter, but scale wider.
- Use real territorial pressures first: mining exhaustion, freight routes, river access, grazing, winter, Indian-country boundaries, Army reach, and territorial politics.
- Keep the chronology locked to 1873. Do not import Little Bighorn, the Great Sioux War, or railroad-built Montana into present tense.
- Keep a hard distinction between verified history, plausible synthesis, and purely fictional composite material.
- Every settlement must be useful at the table in one read.
- Every village file must support lawful play, outlaw play, and morally compromised play.
