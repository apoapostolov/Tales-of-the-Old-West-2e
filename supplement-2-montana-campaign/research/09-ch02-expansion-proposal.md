# Chapter 02 Expansion Proposal

This document proposes a focused expansion pass for [02-the-yellowstone-line.md](/mnt/c/git-public/tales-of-the-old-west-2e/supplement-2-montana-campaign/book-content/02-the-yellowstone-line.md).

The immediate target is to add at least `8+` strong manuscript pages without padding.

The growth should come from:

- better readability inside major and minor scenes
- more room for player freedom at obvious pressure points
- stronger personal and political betrayals
- more side opportunities that feel like Montana rather than generic encounter filler
- two private offers from major NPCs that can split the party or poison trust inside it

## Expansion Goal

The current chapter already has good shape, good pressures, and a workable multilinear spine.

What it still needs is more lived-in scene weight.

Right now many sections tell the GM what the scene means. The next pass should spend more words on what the scene feels like when played, what players may try besides the expected route, and how each scene can break toward greed, fear, loyalty, or private leverage.

That means the extra pages should not come from summary. They should come from:

- richer scene-openers
- more optional beats inside the existing scenes
- more read-aloud text at the start of important turns
- more private agendas inside camp
- more opportunities for the party to fracture under pressure

## Best Places to Add 8+ Pages

These are the highest-value insertion points.

### 1. Fort Ellis opening expansion

Add `1.5-2` pages here.

Purpose:

- make the opening feel less like intake and more like a knot of people already lying
- slow the reader down inside the fort before the violence begins
- let the party start making enemies or allies before the first blood

Additions:

- one more read-aloud block for the wagon yard, sutler room, or officers' side table

  - one with Pike's foreman complaining about short oats and bad loads

### 2. Bell killing investigation expansion

Add `1-1.5` pages here.

Purpose:

- make Bell's death feel like a true opening rupture
- improve readability by turning clues into more playable moments

Additions:

- one short read-aloud block for Bell's body and the silence around it
- a paragraph each on:
  - questioning Mercer under pressure
  - questioning Pike's men in a hostile group
  - what Rowe does if the party tries to arrest someone immediately
- a subsection called `If the Party Treats This Like Murder, Not Prelude`
- more consequences for beating answers out of people, posting their own guard, or locking down the dispatch chest

### 3. Calder Ranch as a full moral stop

Add `1-1.5` pages here.

Purpose:

- make Hannah Calder more than one useful ally scene
- give the chapter one stronger civilian locus before the expedition turns fully inward
- add drama that is not just bullets and paper

Additions:

- one more read-aloud block for evening at the ranch

  - leaves a witness at the ranch
  - caches papers there

  - asks Hannah to testify later

### 4. Route fork as a party fracture scene

Add `1-1.5` pages here.

Add `1.5-2` pages here.

## Two Private Offers That Can Break the Party

These should not be generic temptations. They should be dangerous because each can be rationally defended by a player character.

They should be delivered privately or nearly privately. If heard by the whole party, they lose much of their poison.

### Offer One: Silas Vane's Quiet Future

Best placement:

- late `Camp After the Attack`
- or `Camp of Accusations`

Pitch:

Vane approaches the player he judges most ambitious, most educated, most indebted, or most practical. He does not ask for open betrayal. He asks for selection.

He offers:

- real money now
- a later introduction in Helena or Bozeman
- protection from Army blame if the expedition collapses
- a promise that one packet can disappear while the rest still reaches official hands

What he wants:

- one copied route set
- one warning before the party turns anything over to Rowe
- one name inside the party: who can be bought, who cannot, who is carrying what

Why it is dangerous:

- a player can justify taking it as prudence rather than treason
- partial cooperation with Vane does not require immediate cartoon villainy
- if discovered, it can split the party between survivalists and loyalists

Best twist:

Vane offers not only money, but status. He tells the chosen character that Montana will belong to men who know where to stand before the rail comes, and that courage without position is just another way to die poor.

### Offer Two: Rowe's Unofficial Authority

Best placement:

- after Keene
- or after the ford
- strongest in `Camp of Accusations`

Pitch:

Rowe privately approaches the player he sees as most reliable under pressure. He offers no money. He offers legitimacy.

He asks that player to act as his unofficial hand inside the camp.

He offers:

- quiet legal shelter if the chapter ends badly
- future Army work
- the right to move, search, or hold under Rowe's implied authority
- eventual public backing if hard choices become ugly

What he wants:

- the truth about Vane
- the truth about Reed if necessary
- the truth about what the rest of the party is holding back
- the packet, or at least first notice before the packet moves

Why it is dangerous:

- a player can justify it as the only way to keep order
- it encourages secrecy inside the party in the name of duty
- it can put one character in the position of spying on allies while still believing he is the only adult in the camp

Best twist:

Rowe does not frame this as betrayal. He frames it as burden. He makes the player feel chosen because the others may be brave, clever, or useful, but not steady.

### Optional Third Offer: Shows-the-Ford's Memory

Best placement:

- immediately after private contact
- or later, through LaRoche

Pitch:

Shows-the-Ford offers the player most likely to understand consequence a bargain that sounds cleaner than it is.

He asks for:

- one packet destroyed
- one line left unmarked
- one promise of warning if survey men return

He offers:

- safer passage now
- one later Crow contact
- memory instead of payment

Why it is dangerous:

- it may be morally better than the white offers and still split the party just as badly
- it pits immediate survival and local justice against evidence, leverage, and future profit

## Minor Scene Upgrades

These are smaller additions that improve readability and freedom without changing the chapter's structure.

### Add a short closing paragraph to each numbered scene

Many scenes already open well, but some still end as instruction rather than aftermath.

Each numbered section should end with one short paragraph answering:

- what changes in the camp or column after this
- who now mistrusts whom
- what new pressure becomes visible

That alone will make the scenario read more smoothly.

### Add one clear player-freedom paragraph to each numbered scene

Not a bullet dump. One paragraph.

Pattern:

- what players may try that the obvious version of the scene does not assume
- what the likely cost is
- what kind of reward or leverage may come from it

This will make the chapter feel permissive without turning into procedure.

### Add more read-aloud blocks

Best targets:

- Bell's body
- leaving Fort Ellis
- dusk at Calder Ranch
- the route fork
- the first field markers
- the ford before the attack starts
- the first hour of the return to settlement
- the room where the papers are first opened

These should be short, physical, and easy to read aloud.

## Political Backstabbery to Strengthen

The chapter already has corruption. What it needs is more active betrayal timing.

Add:

- a moment where Pike and Vane are clearly not aligned as neatly as they pretend
- a moment where Reed is tempted to conceal something because the technical truth may still ruin him
- a moment where Harlan may lie out of pride rather than greed
- a moment where Duffy knows more than he says because he is trying to keep the column from breaking before dawn

This makes the intrigue less binary. It should not feel like honest Army and honest survey on one side, dirty money on the other. Every power center should have its own crack in it.

## Best Single New Scene

If only one fully new scene is added, it should be this:

### The Night of Separate Fires

Placement:

- between `Camp After the Attack` and `Tracks and Shadowing`
- or between `Camp of Accusations` and `The Copied Packet`

Content:

The camp has effectively broken into several fire circles.

At one:

- Rowe and Duffy talk order, desertion, and how much of this can still be brought home

At another:

- Vane speaks softly with one player or one frightened clerk

At another:

- Pike's men talk about freight, losses, and whether they are still being paid enough to die here

At another:

- Reed keeps working like a man who believes stopping would be the same as surrender

Why this scene matters:

- it gives the GM a clean place to distribute private offers
- it makes camp politics spatial and readable
- it lets the party split up by choice and thereby create new mistrust among themselves

## Recommended Next Writing Pass

If the goal is one efficient high-value pass, do the following in order:

1. Expand `Camp of Accusations` and `The Copied Packet` with the private offers from Vane and Rowe.
2. Deepen the `Route Fork` so party division there feels politically serious.
3. Expand Calder Ranch with one stronger domestic-local scene.
4. Add the `Night of Separate Fires`.
5. Expand the reckoning into multiple first-room options and cross-institution evidence play.

That pass should add the requested `8+` pages without filler and materially improve the chapter.
