# Montana 1873 - Audit of the Core Thesis

This file integrates the user-provided Montana analysis and audits it against year-specific historical evidence.

## Bottom Line

Montana Territory is still the strongest fit for the kind of systemic sandbox this supplement wants. That part of the thesis holds. The corrections are about chronology and emphasis, not about the choice of territory.

Montana in 1873 gives the game:

- a sparse and dangerous settlement web
- a mining belt already moving from easy rush to consolidation and decline
- a major river freight artery at Fort Benton
- active Army posts but weak territorial reach
- Native nations as strategic powers, not scenery
- vigilante memory and extra-legal violence baked into public life
- pre-railroad speculation and survey pressure rather than full railroad occupation

That still produces structural conflict rather than isolated incidents.

## What Holds Up

### 1. Montana is a contested extraction frontier

This holds. Montana Territory was created in 1864 during the gold-rush years, and the Montana Historical Society notes that by 1870 the non-Indian population was only 20,595. That is enough people to create competing centers of wealth and violence, but not enough institutional depth to stabilize the territory.

### 2. Low density and distance favor sandbox play

This holds strongly. Fort Benton mattered because of Missouri steamboat access; Bozeman, Deer Lodge, Virginia City, Helena, Bannack, and western valley settlements were materially connected but still separated by dangerous roads, mountain weather, and slow communication. That is ideal for sandbox play.

### 3. Native nations must be active strategic actors

This absolutely holds. The NPS material on the Bozeman Trail and Yellowstone country makes clear that Montana and its approaches remained militarized borderlands shaped by Lakota, Cheyenne, Crow, Blackfeet, Salish, Pend d'Oreille, Kootenai, Assiniboine, and Gros Ventre power. A Montana supplement that treats Native nations as background would be historically false.

### 4. Vigilante logic belongs in the setting

This holds, but as residue and precedent rather than a single fresh event. Montana Historical Society materials show the vigilante tradition was foundational and remained politically useful as a language of order, threat, and legitimacy. In 1873 that means formal law exists, but many people still believe force outside the courts is acceptable.

## What Needs Correction

### 1. Little Bighorn is not part of 1873 Montana's present tense

This is the biggest correction. The Battle of the Little Bighorn happened on June 25-26, 1876, during the Great Sioux War. In 1873, that war has not happened yet.

What belongs in 1873 instead:

- the memory of Red Cloud's War and the failure of the Bozeman Trail military road
- Yellowstone exploration and military escort
- the Yellowstone Expedition of 1873, during which the 7th Cavalry fought Lakota warriors
- active uncertainty over how far military-backed expansion can safely push into contested country

So the setting should feel like the run-up to a larger war, not the aftermath of one.

### 2. Railroad power in 1873 is indirect in Montana

The user thesis is right that railroad expansion pressure matters, but wrong if read as completed railroad rule in Montana in 1873.

The Library of Congress notes:

- by 1873 the Northern Pacific had reached Bismarck, North Dakota
- the Yellowstone Expedition of 1873 was tied to surveying a route along the Yellowstone
- the Panic of 1873 damaged the company hard

So for this supplement, railroad pressure in 1873 should mean:

- survey parties
- land speculation
- route lobbying
- political bribery
- Army-backed reconnaissance
- local boosters trying to position towns for future advantage

Not yet:

- rail towns across Montana
- full railroad labor corridors
- complete corporate-state domination of Montana settlements

### 3. Helena is not yet the territorial capital

In 1873, Virginia City is still the territorial capital. Montana Historical Society's capital timeline puts the move to Helena in 1875.

This matters because it sharpens the setting:

- Virginia City has formal political legitimacy
- Helena has stronger commercial gravity
- the tension between legal capital and money capital is useful campaign fuel

### 4. The economic picture should not over-center later copper

For 1873, the supplement should focus first on:

- Bannack / Grasshopper country
- Virginia City / Alder Gulch
- Helena / Last Chance Gulch and its surrounding mining-commercial network
- Diamond City / Confederate Gulch
- Philipsburg / Flint Creek
- freight, stock, timber, hay, and supply economies that feed those places

Later railroad-linked and copper-king Montana belongs to later chapters of state history, not to the baseline feel of 1873.

## Year-Accurate Core Model for the Supplement

The strongest historical model for Montana in 1873 is this:

- weak but no longer absent territorial government
- real but incomplete Army reach
- capital and law not aligned in the same town
- mining camps aging into oligarchic settlements
- river freight still critical
- cattle formalizing through brands and valley wintering systems
- Native territorial power still active and often decisive
- railroad survey pressure entering politics before rails enter geography
- outlaw bands thriving in the seams between all of the above

## Design Consequences

### Recommended central tensions

Use these instead of a single simple frontier binary:

1. capital versus commerce
2. formal law versus vigilante habit
3. Army reach versus actual control
4. Native territorial power versus settler expansion
5. old placer wealth versus new capital consolidation
6. river freight present versus railroad future

### Recommended campaign feel

Players should feel they are operating inside an unstable territorial system, not a neat story engine.

The right phrase is:

`pre-consolidation Montana`

That means:

- institutions exist, but cannot dominate the whole territory
- violence is structural, but not evenly distributed
- every route and settlement has a different power mixture
- the map feels unfinished
