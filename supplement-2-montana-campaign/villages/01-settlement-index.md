# Montana Settlement Index

This is the first-pass settlement map for the supplement.

## Major Settlements

| Settlement | Why It Matters in 1873 | Core Pressure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Virginia City | Territorial capital in 1873, Alder Gulch wealth, old vigilante shadows | decline, power hoarding, formal legitimacy under strain |
| Helena | Strongest urban-commercial center in the mining belt, rich enough to overshadow the capital before formally replacing it in 1875 | finance, freight, corruption, capital ambition |
| Fort Benton | Missouri freight choke point and robe-trade hinge | river control, smuggling, border pressure |
| Deer Lodge | Valley agriculture, road junction, prison and court gravity | transport, law, stock, local elites |
| Bozeman | Gallatin farm-and-stock hub on dangerous frontier approaches | growth, Army dependence, Indian-country edge |
| Missoula | Western valley node with mission legacy, timber, and pass traffic | isolation, trade, diplomacy, land pressure |
| Bannack | Old gold camp still useful as a hard, diminished mining center | exhaustion, crime memory, remoteness |

## Moderate Settlements

| Settlement | Why It Matters in 1873 | Core Pressure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Diamond City | Confederate Gulch after the first rush, still volatile | water, exhausted placers, speculation |
| Philipsburg | Flint Creek mining and timber support town | lode development, labor, freight |
| Radersburg | South Boulder mining settlement with ranch support around it | thin law, unstable ore hopes |
| Stevensville | Mission-valley agricultural community | land transition, mixed authority, west-valley tension |
| Sun River Crossing | Crossing, hay, stock, and fort-orbit logistics on the northern line | transport, Blackfeet pressure, military dependence |
| Blackfoot City | Near-Helena camp-and-road settlement with vice and turnover | parasitic growth, crime, short-horizon money |
| Argenta | Beaverhead mining satellite tied to Bannack-country fortunes | remoteness, ore quality, labor scarcity |

## Promotion Candidates

- If one moderate settlement develops richer conflict than Bannack or Missoula during writing, promote it.
- Sun River Crossing may become a broader `Sun River / Fort Shaw corridor` chapter if military and Blackfeet-country pressures demand it.
- Blackfoot City may merge into a Helena satellite section if it proves too small for a standalone entry.

## Scope Rule

The product should not stop at these 14 settlements. The current working target is:

- `7` major settlement chapters
- `14` moderate settlement entries
- `30` minor named nodes in the atlas and appendices

That is the right scale for a territory sandbox without inventing a dense fake Montana.
