# Missoula

`Status:` Skeleton

Missoula is a western-valley node shaped by river confluence, mission legacy, timber, farming, and the traffic of men moving through mountain passes. It should feel greener, more insulated, and yet politically unsettled in ways different from the mining belt.

### Resource and Geology Notes

- Missoula should be built around valley abundance relative to other Montana settlements: more timber, more water, more arable support, but harder overland connections and weaker concentrated capital.

### Factions in Missoula

- Economic: mill owners, farmers, stock men, traders, mule skinners
- Military / law: local sheriff, road patrols, ad hoc posses
- Native: Salish, Pend d'Oreille, Kootenai diplomacy and pressure
- Political: mission legacy families, newcomers, valley land claimants
