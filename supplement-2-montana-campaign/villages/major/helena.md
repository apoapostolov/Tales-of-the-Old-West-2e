# Helena

`Status:` Skeleton

Helena sits in the heart of the west-central mining belt and is the closest thing Montana Territory has to a commercial capital in 1873: banks, merchants, teamsters, claims men, lawyers, vice, and freight all converging on a town whose easy placers are fading but whose influence is not.

### Resource and Geology Notes

- Helena should be written as a place sustained not just by nearby gold but by the larger mineral belt around it, by trade, finance, and by its pull over nearby camps and supply routes.

### Factions in Helena

- Economic: bankers, mine owners, merchants, freight contractors
- Military / law: territorial officials, sheriffs, prison-connected interests, private guards
- Native: indirect through trade, agencies, and territorial policy
- Political: capital lobby, courthouse bloc, anti-monopoly reformers
