# Bozeman

`Status:` Skeleton

Bozeman sits in the Gallatin Valley where farming, hay, horses, and trail traffic meet a hard frontier. It should be written as a place that wants to become respectable but still depends on military protection and on routes that pass through contested country.

### Resource and Geology Notes

- The Gallatin is one of the best agricultural anchors in the territory. Bozeman should therefore feel materially stronger than many mining towns, but much more vulnerable to raids, road closures, and military failure.

### Factions in Bozeman

- Economic: farmers, stock men, teamsters, merchants, road houses
- Military / law: Fort Ellis orbit, sheriff, scouts
- Native: Crow interests, Lakota and Cheyenne pressure farther east and southeast
- Political: valley boosters, security hardliners, anti-Army taxpayers
