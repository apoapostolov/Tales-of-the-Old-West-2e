## Chapter 15 - The Standoff

> _EPIGRAPH PLACEHOLDER — a line about the silence before the gun, the weight of a decision nobody wants to be first to make._

### Status: Scaffolded

This chapter will be developed into full manuscript prose at a later pass. What follows is the ideation the development will be built on.

### What This Chapter Is For

The duel (corebook Ch.5) is a formal 1-on-1 agreed contest with a structured draw. The **standoff** is its unstable, multi-party cousin — three or four people with hands on their six-guns, nobody willing to be first and nobody willing to back down, the room holding its breath. It is the most-played Western scene that currently has no rules home: Book 4 references a "Standoff or Siege" *operation* (in the justice and train-robbery chapters), but that is an abstraction for capturing a fortified fugitive, not a scene-level mechanic for the tension itself.

### Core Ideas (to be developed)

- **The Standoff Round.** A short procedure distinct from combat Rounds — a courage/bluff resolution that decides who breaks first, who loses nerve, who flinches. Likely an opposed `Quick` + `Presence`/`InsIGHT` roll, or a card-draw parallel to the initiative deck that matches the period feel.
- **Break-points table.** A small D66 or D6 table of how the standoff resolves: someone draws first (into the combat Round) / someone backs down (de-escalation, with a `Standing`/`Reputation` cost) / a third party intervenes / it fizzles into an uneasy peace. Each result should ladder cleanly into either the duel rules, the combat Round, or the social-conflict rules.
- **The modifiers.** `Quick`, reputation, the `Cold Blooded` talent, the `Quick Draw` talent, the `Man's Best Friend` or `Companion` presence, the `Lawyer`'s cool — all should bear on the roll.
- **Laddering, not replacing.** The standoff is the door into the fight, not the fight. It resolves the *whether* and the *who first*, then hands off to the existing combat/duel/social rules.

### What It Will Lean On

The corebook's initiative deck (Ch.5), duels (Ch.5), `Cold Blooded` / `Quick Draw` talents (Ch.4), and the social-conflict rules (Ch.5). It resolves a contradiction the audit flagged: Book 4's "Standoff or Siege" operation cross-reference currently points at nothing scene-level.
