## Chapter 17 - Mail and the Post

> _EPIGRAPH PLACEHOLDER — a line about the mail being the county's only regular visitor from the wider world, and the things it carries besides letters._

### Status: Scaffolded

This chapter will be developed into full manuscript prose at a later pass.

### What This Chapter Is For

The U.S. Mail is referenced everywhere — the `Express Mail Route` amenity, the federal-mail hanging offense in train robbery, Wells Fargo riders carrying "payroll, mail, and valuables," the Star Route fraud, Mary Fields as a star-route postwoman — but there is no postal subsystem. No delivery-time table, no mail-cost, no "mail as the carrier of news/money/legal documents across the county," no way for a PC to *be* a mail carrier or to disrupt the mail as a score.

### Core Ideas (to be developed)

- **The Post Table.** Mail routes as way-station-spanning lines (hooks the trade chapter's spatial economy, Ch.9); delivery times by route type (star route, stage, rail); cost; the federal-reach escalation hook (mail robbery = federal crime, as train robbery Ch.5 establishes).
- **The mail as cargo.** Letters, legal documents, news, payroll, specie — the mail carries more than words, and the chapter should let it. A "what's in the mailbag" angle that bridges to the train-robbery express car.
- **The mail as score and vocation.** A short operation frame ("the mail run," "the mail robbery," "the star-route swindle") parallel to the train and bank jobs. The mail carrier as a PC vocation with its own Trouble (weather, the road, the raid).
- **The mail contract as faction lever.** The star-route contract as a patron the robber barons (Ch.13) and factions (Ch.2) fight over.

### What It Will Lean On

The trade chapter's spatial economy (Ch.9), the train-robbery chapter's federal-mail rules (Ch.5), the robber-baron chapter (Ch.13, the contract as patron), the corebook's `Express Mail Route` amenity, and the way-station/keeper frames (Ch.9/10).
