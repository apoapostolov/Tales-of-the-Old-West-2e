## Chapter 16 - The Wire

> _THE WIRE IS FASTER THAN ANY HORSE AND IT DOES NOT TIRE AND IT DOES NOT FORGET. THE MAN WHO CUTS IT BUYS HIMSELF A DAY. THE MAN WHO DOESN'T BUYS THE COUNTY'S ATTENTION AT THE SPEED OF CURRENT._
>
> — DUTCH MILLER, TO A FIREMAN WHO ASKED WHY THE WHISTLE MATTERED

### The Message Outruns the Horse

The telegraph is the new thing in the West, newer than the railroad in most counties, and it has changed the country more quietly and more completely than the iron horse. A wire sent from the railhead in the morning is in Santa Fe by noon and in Washington by the evening, and the man who robbed the morning train is on a warrant in three territories before his horse is cool. The wire is the reason a clean getaway stopped being clean. It is the reason the posse knows your name before you do.

Whenever the wire matters, settle three things fast: what the message costs, how quickly it arrives, and who can cut, delay, read, or misuse it. The train robbery's pursuit, the justice chapter's warrant, the faction's pressure, and the baron's reach all use that same table reality.

> _Miller had the fireman hold the boiler while he walked back to the station agent's window with the way-bill and the message he had written on the back of it in the careful print of a man who had been a telegrapher before he was an engineer. The agent read the message and read it again and looked at Miller._
>
> "You want this sent to the marshal's office in Las Vegas."
>
> "I want it sent to the marshal's office in Las Vegas, and I want the priority stamp on it, and I want it before the southbound leaves this platform."
>
> "The priority stamp is a dollar a word."
>
> "I know what the priority stamp is. Send the wire."
>
> _The agent counted the words and counted the dollar a word and wrote the figure on the slip and pushed it across the counter. Miller paid it. The wire went out at twelve-fourteen, which was four minutes before the southbound left the platform, and by two o'clock the marshal in Las Vegas knew the name of the man who had stood Miller's train on the curve, and by sundown the name was on a wire to the territorial office in Santa Fe. That was the wire's work. That was what a dollar a word bought, in the country the wire had made._

### The Wire Table

A telegraph is sent from a telegraph office, which is a town amenity (the corebook's `Telegraph` amenity, or a railroad depot with an operator). A wire's cost and speed are set by the distance from the office to the receiving office and the priority the sender puts on it.

| Wire | Cost | Transmission Time | What It Buys |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Local (within the county) | `10¢` per word | minutes to an hour | The wire is read the same day |
| Regional (territory-wide) | `25¢` per word | hours | The wire is read within the day |
| Federal (to Washington or coast to coast) | `50¢` per word | a day | The wire crosses the continent |
| Priority stamp (any distance) | `+$1` per word | halved | The operator bumps it to the front of the queue; receiving office flags it |

A wire is paid by the word, and the period counted every word — the sender learned to write tight. A typical wire is ten to twenty words. A long one, fifty. The priority stamp is the operator's surcharge to jump the queue and the receiving office's flag, and it is the thing a railroad, a marshal, or a baron pays to make the wire land first.

#### The Minimum Word

An office will not send a wire under five words, because the operator's clerical fee is in those words. _Arrive Tuesday stop. Bring cash stop._ is eight words and a complete errand. _Bandits hit the 9:14 at the Cimarron curve, three riders, two rifles, heading south_ is fifteen words and a federal warrant by sundown.

### The Office and the Operator

The telegraph office is the wire's door, and the operator is the man who holds the key. An office is a small room with a sounder, a key, a battery, and a chair, and it is staffed by a man who hears the county's business in the click of the sounder before the county hears it in the newspaper. The operator is a low-grade faction contact of the kind the factions chapter describes — not a power, but a listener, and a listener with a price.

| The Operator | What They Are to the Party |
| --- | --- |
| The company man | Loyal to the railroad or the company; reads the wire's traffic to the bosses. `Standing` with the railroad faction (the train robbery chapter). |
| the federal clerk | A territorial or federal appointee; reads the wire's traffic to the marshal or the bench. `Standing` with the justice system (the justice chapter). |
| The local hire | A county boy with a key and a wage; reads the wire's traffic to whoever is buying, which is often everyone. A `Refuge`-style contact (the outlaw chapter). |

An operator's regard for the party is tracked the way a settlement's `Refuge` is tracked, on a small `-2` to `+2` scale, and it sets the modifier on any roll to read the wire's traffic, to send a wire the company would rather not send, to delay a wire the company would rather send fast, or to learn what the wire has been saying about the party. An operator at `+2` is the party's ear on the county's nerve; an operator at `-2` is the party's name on every wire the company sends.

#### The Operator as an Influence Contact

The operator's regard is a small **Influence pool** (cap `5`), using the factions chapter's system (Ch.13). The party earns it by public acts the operator notices — a Christmas bottle, a favor done in the office, company regard paid in the operator's direction — and spends it to send a wire the company would rather refuse, delay one the company wants fast, or read the traffic clicking through the sounder. Influence cools at each Turn of the Season the party has not cultivated the relationship. The operator is a person, not a switch.

### Cutting the Wire

The wire runs on poles along the railroad and the stage road, and a man with an axe or a climber's spikes can take it down. Cutting the wire is the outlaw's and the soldier's oldest trick — deny the county its speed, and the county is as slow as a horse again.

Cutting the wire is a single check, with the ability and the consequence set by how it is done.

| The Cut | The Ability | The Consequence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Axe a pole, drop the line | `LABOR` | Fast, loud, obvious. The company knows within the hour which pole went down; a line crew is out by the next morning. |
| Climb and clip the line | `OPERATE` or `LIGHT-FINGERED` | Slower, quieter. The company knows the line is dead but not where; the line crew must walk the poles to find it. |
| Tap the line, send a dummy | `OPERATE` (with a stolen key or the `Engineer` talent) | The wire stays up and carries the message the sender wants it to carry. The company does not know the line is compromised until the dummy and the truth collide downstream. |
| Take the office | `PRESENCE`/`SHOOTIN'` (the operator at gunpoint) | The wire stays up but sends nothing the party does not approve. The company knows within the hour; the troop or the posse is on the wire's loss. |

A failed cut leaves the wire up and the cutter exposed, which is the worst of both. A cut that succeeds buys the party the time the wire would have stolen — the pursuit that does not leave today, the warrant that does not reach the next county, the baron's order that does not arrive. The wire-down condition below sets how long the buy lasts.

### The Wire-Down Condition

When the wire is cut, jammed, or held, the county enters a **wire-down** condition. The wire-down is a pressure on the county's timelines, and it lasts until a line crew or an operator restores the service.

| Wire-Down Cause | How Long It Lasts |
| --- | --- |
| A pole axed (obvious) | `1D6` Shifts — the line crew finds it and fixes it in a day or two |
| The line clipped (hidden) | `1D6+1` Shifts — the line crew must walk the poles to find it |
| The office held | Until the holders leave or the troop arrives |
| The dummy message | Until the dummy and the truth collide, which is `1D6` Shifts to `1D6` days, by how far the messages must travel to meet |
| A storm (the weather chapter) | Until the storm passes and the crew can work |

While the wire is down, the county's timelines slow to horse-speed. Pursuit that would have been `Regional` by wire is `Local` until the wire returns. A warrant that would have reached the next county today reaches it in three days. A baron's order that would have moved a faction this season does not arrive until the line is up. Let the cut matter by shifting every timeline the wire would have compressed.

### Interception and the Traffic

The wire carries the county's traffic, and the traffic is readable by anyone with an ear on the line or a key to the office. Reading the wire's traffic is an `INSIGHT` or `HAWKEYE` roll, modified by the operator's `Refuge`-style regard (above) and the traffic's opacity.

| What the Reader Wants | The Roll |
| --- | --- |
| The gist of the day's traffic | `INSIGHT`, base pool, `+1 die` if the operator is at `+1` or better |
| A specific wire's content | `INSIGHT` at `-1 die` (the wire is gone the moment it is sent; the reader must catch it in the click) |
| Whether the party's name is on the wire | `INSIGHT` base pool, modified by the operator's regard |
| The wire's source and destination | `HAWKEYE` — reading the routing, not the content |

A failed read is a wrong guess or a stale one. A successful read is the party's ear on the county's nerve, which is often worth more than the dollar a word the wire cost to send.

#### Example: Buying a Day

The gang knows a warrant will leave the railhead by wire at noon. They choose to **climb and clip the line** instead of axing a pole. The roll is `LIGHT-FINGERED`; it succeeds. The wire-down condition lasts `1D6+1` Shifts because the line crew must walk the poles to find the break.

For that day, pursuit stays at horse-speed. The marshal in the next county does not get the warrant until the line is fixed, and the gang's `Regional` trouble remains local long enough for them to cross the dry wash. But the company now knows the line was cut, and the operator's regard cools because the break happened on his watch. The cut bought time. It did not erase consequence.

### The Wire and the Other Rules

The wire is a thread that runs through half the book, and this chapter is the knot that ties the thread to the table.

- **The train robbery chapter** leans on the wire as the pursuit accelerant — the wire that ruins a clean getaway. The cut and the dummy (above) are the mechanics that chapter already needs, and a gang that cuts the wire or holds the office before a score is a gang that buys itself the head start the wire would have stolen.
- **The justice chapter** leans on the wire as the way a warrant reaches the troop — the wire goes to the post. The wire-down condition says the warrant does not arrive today, and the troop does not ride until the line is up.
- **The factions and robber-barons chapters** lean on the wire as the medium a baron's pressure travels through — a name on a wire, by Thursday. The operator is the baron's ear, and a party that owns the operator owns a piece of the baron's reach.
- **The mail chapter (the next chapter)** leans on the wire as the fast cousin of the mail — the wire for the urgent, the mail for the durable.
- **The town tracker** treats a working telegraph as civic infrastructure. A season spent winning or building reliable service may make the `Telegraph`, `Railroad Station`, `Post Office`, or `Federal Marshal's Office` amenity available at `3` SP. A cut line that costs the town a season's trade, law, or rescue may remove `1` `Mercantile`, `Law`, or `Civic` tally point.

The wire is the country's nervous system. Once its cost, speed, and failure are known, every warrant, robbery, faction order, and rescue call moves at the same hard pace.
