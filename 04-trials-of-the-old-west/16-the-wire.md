## Chapter 16 - The Wire

> _EPIGRAPH PLACEHOLDER — a line about the wire being faster than any horse, and the county learning its trouble at the speed of current._

### Status: Scaffolded

This chapter will be developed into full manuscript prose at a later pass.

### What This Chapter Is For

The telegraph is **load-bearing in three Book 4 chapters** — train robbery ("the wire that ruins a clean getaway"), justice ("the wire goes to the post"), factions ("a name on a wire, by Thursday") — yet it is purely narrative. There is no cost table, no speed/distance table, no interception/cut mechanic, no "the wire is down" condition. GMs hand-wave every instance. This chapter gives the wire a small, shared mechanical definition so every chapter that invokes it draws from the same well.

### Core Ideas (to be developed)

- **The Wire Table.** Cost per word by distance from the nearest office; transmission time in hours; the office as a town amenity (hooks the corebook's `Telegraph` amenity). Anchored to the 1870s supplement's communication pricing.
- **Cutting the wire.** A short procedure — an ability check (`OPERATE`, `LIGHT-FINGERED`, or `MAKIN'`) to cut or clip the line, a dummy message sent from a stolen key, and the consequences (the company knows, the pursuit timeline shifts, the county's news goes silent). This is the mechanic the train-robbery and outlaw chapters already need.
- **Interception and the operator.** The operator as a low-level faction contact; reading the wire's traffic as an `INSIGHT`/`HAWKEYE` roll; the wire as a `Standing` lever with the railroad (Ch.5/Ch.13).
- **The wire down.** A "wire down" condition — weather (Ch.3), sabotage, or a cut — and what it does to the county's pursuit, trade, and news timelines.

### What It Will Lean On

The `OPERATE` ability, the train-robbery chapter (Ch.5, the wire as pursuit accelerant), the outlaw chapter's pursuit rules, the faction engine (Ch.2), and the 1870s supplement's communication pricing lore.
