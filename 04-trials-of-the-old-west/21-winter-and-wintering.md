## Chapter 21 - Winter and Wintering

> _EPIGRAPH PLACEHOLDER — a line about the season the country closes, and what it costs the people who winter in it._

### Status: Scaffolded

This chapter will be developed into full manuscript prose at a later pass.

### What This Chapter Is For

The weather chapter (Ch.3) covers the *blizzard disaster* and the drought; the outlaw chapter has an optional `Provisions` die for bands "wintering hard." But there is **no general "wintering" subsystem** for a town, ranch, or homestead — the season between the autumn roundup and the spring drive, when the campaign's economy and movement contract. The Turn of the Season handles spring/summer/fall business; winter is a hole in the campaign calendar.

### Core Ideas (to be developed)

- **The Winter Quarter.** An interlude layered on the Turn of the Season — a provisions/fuel/feed attrition track, the closed pass, the frozen stock, the cabin fever. Winter as the season the country *contracts*.
- **What Winter Brought table.** A D66 table of the season's pressures: the deep snow, the early thaw, the wolf pack at the corral, the stranded traveler at the door (hooks the sickness chapter's quarantine), the cabin-fever quarrel (hooks the family feud and the outlaw cohesion), the frozen stock, the fuel run out.
- **The spring thaw.** The resolution — who survived, what was lost, what the winter did to `Cohesion`/`Horsestock`/`Provisions`/the town's `Welfare`. The thaw as the campaign's annual reset.
- **Winter as the sickness season.** The strongest hook to the sickness chapter (Ch.11) — the diseases that thrived in the close air of the winter cabin and the snowbound camp.

### What It Will Lean On

The weather chapter (Ch.3), the sickness chapter (Ch.11), the conditions (`Freezing`, `Starving`), the optional `Provisions` die, the outlaw `Horsestock`/`Cohesion` gauges, and the Turn of the Season. Winter is the season the rest of the book's pressure gauges all climb at once.
