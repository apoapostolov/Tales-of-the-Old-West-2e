## Chapter 20 - The Holdout (Homestead and Cabin Siege)

> _EPIGRAPH PLACEHOLDER — a line about the cabin being the only fort the frontier had, and the night some men tried to take it._

### Status: Scaffolded

This chapter will be developed into full manuscript prose at a later pass.

### What This Chapter Is For

The mass-combat chapter (Ch.8) has a unit-scale "Siege and the Running Fight," and the justice chapter (Ch.4) has a "Standoff or Siege" *operation abstraction* for capturing a fugitive. Neither covers the **PC-scale homestead defense** — a handful of named defenders barricaded in a cabin overnight against a larger force, with windows and doors, ammunition running low, fire-arrows, the breach, the cellar. It is a different mechanical shape: a tension/attrition track, not a skirmish line. This is the classic "hold the cabin until dawn" scene.

### Core Ideas (to be developed)

- **The Holdout operation.** A Defense track (Progress inverted — the defenders are trying to *hold* while Trouble climbs): breaches, fires, ammunition (the optional `Provisions` die), the wounded, the dawn. Reuses the Progress/Trouble engine with the polarity flipped.
- **Assault tactics table.** A D6/D66 table of how the attackers press: rush the door, set the roof alight, starve them out, the parley/truce, the feint, the rescue column approaching. Each is a Trouble step or a stage the defenders answer.
- **The room-by-room breach.** When the defense track fails, the fight moves indoors — nested combat Rounds using the corebook's conflict rules, room by room, to the cellar or the roof.
- **The dawn.** The holdout's natural clock — relief, exposure (the attackers lose nerve in daylight), or the last stand.

### What It Will Lean On

The Progress/Trouble operation engine (outlaw chapter), the corebook's combat/conflict rules (Ch.5), the optional `Provisions` die, the weather chapter (Ch.3, the night's conditions), and the mass-combat chapter (Ch.8) for when the attackers are a unit rather than a gang.
