## Chapter 18 - Banking and the Vault

> _EPIGRAPH PLACEHOLDER — a line about the bank being the one building in town with a door built to keep the county out, and the county's opinion of that._

### Status: Scaffolded

This chapter will be developed into full manuscript prose at a later pass.

### What This Chapter Is For

The corebook handles *loans* and notes banks were fly-by-night and robbery-prone. There is a `Bank` amenity. But there is **no bank-robbery operation** (parallel to the train-robbery operation) and **no deposit/safekeeping/safety-deposit mechanic**. A PC who wants to stash cash, or rob a vault, has nothing. This is the single most-requested Western score that currently has no procedure — train robbery got a full chapter; its twin got nothing.

### Core Ideas (to be developed)

- **Bank anatomy.** The counter, the vault, the time-lock, the manager/messenger, the back-room safe — parallel to the train chapter's car-by-car anatomy. Each is a target and a problem.
- **Ways in.** The after-hours break-in, the daylight holdup, the inside job, the safe-crack — each a check or short operation, with the safe-crack as a `LIGHT-FINGERED`/`MAKIN'` centerpiece (hooks `Lockpicker`, `Forger`).
- **The robbery as operation.** Scaled to the bank's size (the territory bank, the county-seat bank, the mining-camp strongbox), using the Progress/Trouble engine. The teller, the alarm, the witness, the posse's response time.
- **Deposits and safekeeping.** A deposit rule — cash stored vs. cash carried, with the bank-run/failure risk the corebook hints at. A bank is a place to put Capital, and a place that can fail and take the Capital with it (a Penalty-table entry the corebook's business rules can absorb).
- **Score seeds.** A D66 table parallel to the train-robbery seeds.

### What It Will Lean On

The train-robbery chapter (Ch.5) as the direct template, the outlaw chapter's operation framework, the faction engine (Ch.2, the bank as a faction's instrument), the corebook's `Bank` amenity and Capital rules, and the justice chapter (Ch.4) for the robbery's legal tail.
