## Chapter 19 - Weddings, Funerals, and the Church Service

> _EPIGRAPH PLACEHOLDER — a line about the county gathering for the rites that mark a life, and the scores it settles while it's there._

### Status: Scaffolded

This chapter will be developed into full manuscript prose at a later pass.

### What This Chapter Is For

These are the recurring ceremonial scenes — the wedding, the funeral, the Sunday service or revival — that recur every campaign and are drama engines, yet have no scene frame. The families chapter (Ch.12) has "Allegiances and Marriage" as a tag, not a procedure; funerals are referenced in lore and family traits ("mourns loud / mourns quiet") but have no mechanic; revivals get paragraphs of lore but no scene structure despite being mass social events.

### Core Ideas (to be developed)

- **The Wedding.** A scene-frame (not a full operation): social-conflict + a Capital/`Standing` transfer (the dowry, the family merger) + the objection mechanic (who objects, and what it costs them). The wedding as the engine's strongest allegiance-builder and its most public flashpoint.
- **The Funeral.** A scene-frame: who attends = the faction map revealed; the will/testament read (hooks the inheritance/land-office gap); the grudge surfaced (the feud that uses the graveside as its stage). The funeral as the county's accounting.
- **The Service and the Revival.** A congregation-as-crowd social-conflict using the existing crowd rules; the preacher (a cast-adjacent figure) as the agent; conversion, repentance, and `Standing` shifts with religious factions. The revival as a county-wide event that moves `Fame` and `Reputation`.
- **The rites as clocks.** Each rite as a seasonal anchor in the Turn of the Season — the wedding season, the funeral's timing, the revival circuit.

### What It Will Lean On

The families chapter (Ch.12, marriage as allegiance), the faction engine (Ch.2, religious and family factions), the social-conflict rules (corebook Ch.5), the town aspects (`Civic`, `Welfare`), and the writing skill's `religion-and-faith.md` for the service/revival register.
