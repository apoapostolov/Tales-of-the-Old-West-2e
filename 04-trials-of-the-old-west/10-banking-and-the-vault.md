## Chapter 10 - Banking and the Vault

> _THE BANK IS THE ONE BUILDING IN TOWN WITH A DOOR BUILT TO KEEP THE COUNTY OUT, AND THE COUNTY HAS OPINIONS ABOUT THAT. THE BANKER IS THE MAN BEHIND THE DOOR, AND THE VAULT IS THE REASON THE GANG CAME IN OFF THE RANGE._
>
> — A KEEPER'S WORD, OVERHEARD AT THE BACK BAR

### The Bank and Its Work

The bank is the county's treasury and its temptation. The corebook handles the bank's civil side — loans, the `Bank` amenity, the Capital that flows through a town's stronger market — and it notes that the frontier banks were fly-by-night, robbery-prone, and sometimes the front for a fraud. What the corebook does not give you is the bank as a target: the bank robbery as an operation, the vault as a problem, the safe-crack as a centerpiece, and the deposit and the safety-deposit box as the legitimate services a party might use or abuse. Train robbery got a full chapter. Its twin — the bank job — got nothing. This is the missing half.

This chapter builds the bank robbery as an operation parallel to the train robbery, gives the bank an anatomy the way the train got its cars, and adds the deposit and safekeeping rules the corebook's Capital system implies but does not state. It is an optional framework for groups who want the county's most iconic score on the table.

> _The gang's planner had the bank's layout on the table in the hideout, drawn in pencil on the back of a way-bill, and he was walking the hands through it the way a trail boss walks a crew through a river crossing. The counter here. The manager's cage here. The vault in the back, behind the iron door, with the time-lock that the planner swore he had the combination to because the manager's daughter was sweet on the right hand._
>
> "The time-lock pops at nine. We're in by nine-oh-five. The teller's behind the cage, the manager's in his office, the back door is the way out and the horse-holder is in the alley."
>
> "And the vault?"
>
> "The vault is the question, Jeb. The combination gets us in clean, or the drills get us in loud, or the powder gets us in dead, and the difference is how much noise the county hears and how fast the posse forms. Pick one."
>
> _Jeb looked at the layout and looked at the hands and looked at the clock, which was the bank's clock, which was the county's clock, which was the thing the gang was about to beat or die trying to beat. He picked the combination, because the combination was quiet and quiet was the gang's one advantage against the wire and the posse and the troop the wire would bring. The daughter was the gamble. The daughter was always the gamble._

### The Anatomy of a Bank

A bank is a small building with a few rooms, and each room is a different problem for a robber. The GM and the planning gang should know the layout before anyone rolls.

| The Bank's Parts | What It Is | What It Is to a Robber |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The counter and the lobby | The public face; the teller's cage, the customers, the daylight | The entry; the witnesses; the teller's drawer (small cash, the easy take) |
| The teller's cage | The rail and the teller behind it; the drawer; the alarm bell | The first take and the first alarm — pull the bell and the county knows |
| The manager's office | The manager, the books, the safe-deposit boxes, the back door | The inside job's door; the books that name the depositors; the boxes that hold the paper |
| The vault | The iron room, the time-lock, the strongboxes, the specie and the paper | The real take; the strongest door in the county; the safe-cracker's work |
| The back room and the alley | The count room, the rear exit, the horse-holder's position | The way out, if the front is hot |

A bank's size and strength track the town's `Mercantile` and `Civic` aspects. A bank in a `Mercantile 2` mining camp is a strongbox in the back of the assayer's office, with a time-lock and a shotgun; a bank in a `Mercantile 5` county seat is a brick building with a vault, two tellers, a manager, and an alarm wire to the marshal's office. Read the bank's strength off the town the way the train robbery chapter reads the run off the line.

### The Robbery as Operation

A bank robbery is an operation, run on the outlaw chapter's Progress-versus-Trouble track, scaled to the bank's size.

| Bank Robbery Scale | Progress Needed | Trouble Limit | The Bank |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `The Strongbox` | `4` | `3` | A camp strongbox, a way-station safe, a single-teller country bank |
| `The County Bank` | `6` | `4` | A county-seat bank with a vault, a manager, and an alarm |
| `The Territorial Bank` | `8` | `5` | A territorial bank with a hardened vault, guards, and a wire to the troop |

**Progress** is securing the take and getting clear. **Trouble** is everything that makes the robbery the county's business: the alarm bell, the witness who slips out the back, the teller who lies about the time-lock, the posse that forms in the saloon across the street, the wire that goes to the marshal (the wire chapter), the gang member who shoots a customer and turns a robbery into a hanging.

| Stage of the Robbery | The Problem | Likely Ability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The entry | Get in, hold the room, keep the witnesses down | `PRESENCE`, `SHOOTIN'` |
| The teller's drawer | Take the easy cash; silence the alarm | `LIGHT-FINGERED`, `PRESENCE` |
| The manager and the boxes | The inside man, the combination, the back door | `INSIGHT`, `PRESENCE` (the manager's nerve) |
| The vault | The time-lock, the combination, the drills, the powder | `LIGHT-FINGERED`, `MAKIN'` (the safe-crack, below) |
| The get clear | Out the back, onto the horses, gone before the posse forms | `MOVE`, `ANIMAL HANDLIN'` |

Each stage is tagged Safe / Risky / Desperate, as the outlaw chapter tags a score. A clean entry with an inside man and the right combination is Safe; a daylight holdup of a county bank with no inside man and a time-locked vault is Desperate, and the Trouble will tell.

#### The Safe-Crack

The vault is the bank's heart, and the safe-crack is the robber's centerpiece. The vault's defense is the time-lock (a clockwork mechanism that holds the bolts until a set hour, immune to combination and threat), the combination lock (which a man with the numbers or the ear can defeat), and the door itself (which a man with drills and time and noise can defeat, or a man with powder can defeat faster and louder).

| The Approach | The Ability | The Cost |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The combination (inside man, manager's nerve, stolen numbers) | `INSIGHT` or `PRESENCE` to get it; no roll to use it | Quiet; the gamble is the source |
| The ear (the `Lockpicker` talent, a stethoscope, a quiet room) | `LIGHT-FINGERED` | Quiet; slow; the `Lockpicker` is the centerpiece |
| The drills (a crew, a quiet hour, the right bits) | `MAKIN'` | Loud enough to carry; slow; the gang is exposed the whole time |
| The powder (the `Forger` or `Engineer` talent, a charge) | `MAKIN'` or `OPERATE` | Loud; the county hears it; the vault may survive and the door may kill the powder man |
| The time-lock's patience | none — the gang waits | The gang is in the bank until the lock opens, with the county outside |

A safe-crack that fails is a vault that holds, and the gang must take the teller's drawer and run, or settle in for a siege the holdout chapter (Ch.2) resolves. A safe-crack that succeeds is the take the gang came for, and the difference between a robbery that pays and one that was a waste of the rope it will cost.

### Deposits and Safekeeping

A bank is also a place to put money, and the corebook's Capital system implies a deposit mechanic it does not state. A player character or a business may deposit cash in a bank for safekeeping, converting carried cash to banked cash, and the bank's strength sets the cost and the risk.

| The Deposit | What It Is | The Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A demand deposit (checking) | Cash held by the bank, drawn by note | The bank can fail (see below); the bank's books are a record the law can read |
| A time deposit (savings) | Cash held for a term, at interest | Higher yield, less access; the bank fails and the term is lost |
| A safety-deposit box | A box in the vault for paper, deeds, valuables | The bank's vault is the safest place in the county — until it is robbed, and the box goes with the take |
| A bank draft | A note drawn on the bank, payable at another bank | The draft is as good as the bank that issued it and the bank that honors it; a failed bank's drafts are wallpaper |

A deposit is a trade: safety for the risk of the bank. The frontier banks were not the institutions the twentieth century built. They were undercapitalized, overextended, and sometimes fraudulent, and a bank run or a failure could take a county's savings in an afternoon.

#### The Bank Run and the Failure

A bank fails when its depositors lose faith and demand their cash at once, or when its fraud is exposed, or when its capital is robbed past its reserves. The Turn of the Season's Fortune roll may produce a bank failure as a town event, and the trade chapter's scarcity and the robber-baron chapter's machinations can produce one by the campaign's pressures. When a bank fails:

- Deposits are frozen, then haircut, then gone, by the bank's reserves. A depositor recovers `D6 × 10%` of the deposit, at best, and often nothing.
- Safety-deposit boxes are seized by the receiver or the robbers, and the paper in them is the county's gossip until the receiver sorts it.
- Drafts are worthless. A character holding a failed bank's draft holds paper.
- The town's `Mercantile` and `Welfare` aspects drop, as the county's savings evaporate and the county's trust with them.

A bank run is a scene the GM may run at the table — the rumor, the line at the door, the depositor who gets his cash and the one who does not, the manager who locks the door and the one who bolts out the back. The engine's job is to make the deposit a trade with a risk the depositor understood, and the failure the cost the depositor sometimes paid.

### The Bank and the Other Rules

The bank ties the book's commerce and crime threads together.

- **The train robbery chapter** is the bank job's template — the anatomy, the operation scales, the score seeds, the federal reach. A gang that robs trains is a gang that will rob banks, and the procedures are parallel.
- **The corebook's Capital rules** are the bank's civil side — the loan, the investment, the property. This chapter is the bank's criminal side, and the deposit rule bridges the two.
- **The wire chapter** defines the alarm wire and the marshal's response time, which the bank robbery's Trouble leans on.
- **The justice chapter** defines the robbery's legal tail — the `Felony` or `Capital` charge, the trial, the sentence, the rope.
- **The robber baron chapter** holds the bank as a faction's instrument — the baron's bank is the baron's lever, and the gang that robs it has made a baron's enemy.

The bank is the county's treasury and its temptation, and a chapter that gives the vault a door and the deposit a risk gives the gang the score the train robbery chapter promised was waiting. The bank job is the outlaw's other great set-piece, and the engine's job is to make it pay what it should pay and cost what it should cost, by the bank's strength and the gang's nerve and the county's memory of the morning the vault door stood open and the county's savings walked out of it on a horse.
