# Book 04 Rules Balance Pass

This pass reviews the numbered Book 04 manuscript as a set of playable table procedures, not as topic coverage. Each chapter was checked against the local West design standards: a rule needs a trigger, player decision, roll or reckoning step, consequence, and next state. Each chapter was also checked through the four balance lenses: mathematical, perceived, table, and campaign balance.

## Method

For each chapter, the review asked:

- What behavior does the rule reward?
- What will players believe the rule rewards?
- What must the GM track or adjudicate?
- Does the rule create a dominant strategy, false choice, free resource, unbounded bonus, or dead-end failure?
- Does failure create playable trouble rather than only stopping the scene?
- Does success create future hooks rather than only payout?
- Does the procedure stay within the game's ordinary dice-pool limits, especially the `+3 dice` ceiling for help and situational advantage?

## System-Wide Fixes Made

- Famine pricing in the trade rules now has a bounded premium and social consequences, so starvation cannot become a pure profit puzzle.
- Influence spending now obeys the `+3 dice` cap, cannot create free Capital, cannot erase capital charges, cannot buy murder, and can secure only ordinary minor favors once per faction per season.
- Market hunting now uses an explicit Decline table keyed to the take die, so heavy harvest has a known ecological and political cost.
- The mass-combat sample unit now follows the `+3 dice` cap instead of presenting a larger bonus and then correcting it in parentheses.
- Chapter 12 language was tightened so the targeted person is never presented as cargo, commodity, or a price-optimized resource.

## Remaining Watch Points

- The enterprise chapters deliberately create campaign-scale power: money, land, faction favor, and labor. They remain safe only if the GM charges upkeep, attention, debt, and social consequence every season.
- The faction and family chapters are strongest when favors are public. Private favors should improve relationships in the fiction but should not create Influence unless witnesses make the act count.
- The grim historical chapters should continue to reward rescue, refusal, exposure, testimony, and repair. They should never reward exploitation with clean profit.
- Examples of play should be checked whenever a rule changes. An example that contradicts the procedure will teach the table the wrong rule faster than the rule text can correct it.

## Publication Verdict

The current Book 04 rules are no longer merely lists of possible settings or subject notes. Each major chapter now has a runnable table loop, visible player choices, consequence hooks, and a next-state for campaign play. The manuscript still needs final polish against the Book 02 voice benchmark and Book 01 rules terminology, but the largest balance risks found in this pass have been corrected in the chapter text.
