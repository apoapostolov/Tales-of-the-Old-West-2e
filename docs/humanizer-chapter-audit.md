# Book 04 Humanizer Audit

This audit records the deep voice pass requested after the first publication rescue work. It uses the full `humanize-professional-prose`, local `avoid-ai-writing`, `stop-slop`, and `western-writing` anti-AI criteria, with special attention to the user's named failures: empty framing, mirrored paragraph structure, repeated "Use these rules when..." constructions, meta-writing, abstract pathos, over-regular paragraph rhythm, comma-heavy listing, and chapter-to-chapter template drift.

The goal of this pass was not to make every paragraph lyrical. Book 04 is a rules supplement, so tables, procedures, cross-references, and exact terms must remain clear. The pass instead removed prose that sounded like an author narrating a design file, and replaced it with book-facing language that tells the GM what changes at the table.

## Checks Applied

- Removed or rewrote throat-clearing openings that announced the book, chapter, or rules before giving the reader useful content.
- Replaced internal or design-process language with table-facing prose.
- Broke repeated explanatory cadences across chapter openings, procedures, examples, and closers.
- Reduced sentence pairs that repeated the same idea in positive and negative form.
- Replaced abstract claims with concrete gameplay pressure: who wants what, what can go wrong, what resource moves, and what consequence reaches the table.
- Preserved useful rules references where they help a GM connect procedures across Book 04, the corebook, and the 1870s sourcebook.
